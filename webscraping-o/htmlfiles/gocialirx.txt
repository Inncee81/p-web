     <h2>
      <span class="ez-toc-section" id="Top_5_Pharmacy_Schools_in_the_U_S">
      </span>
      Top 5 Pharmacy Schools in the U.S.
      <span class="ez-toc-section-end">
      </span>
     </h2>
     <h3>
      1. University of North Carolin – Chapel Hill
     </h3>
     <p>
      There are three distinctive degree programs available at the
      <a href="https://gocialirx.com/">
       University of North Carolina Eshelman School of Pharmacy
      </a>
      :
     </p>
     <ol>
      <li>
       Doctor of Pharmacy
      </li>
      <li>
       Pharm.D. program
      </li>
      <li>
       Ph.D. program
      </li>
     </ol>
     <p>
      The Ph.D. program provides unique research experiences and a specially-tailored curriculum for advanced training in drug development, drug delivery, and drug policy.
     </p>
     <h3>
      2. University of Minnesota
     </h3>
     <p>
      The University of Minnesota’s College of Pharmacy offers a very wide range of programs in the pharmaceutical field. These include:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       A Doctor of Pharmacy
      </li>
      <li>
       Various graduate programs including Medicinal Chemistry and Integrated Biological Sciences
      </li>
      <li>
       Dual Degree Programs
      </li>
      <li>
       A Postgraduate Pharmacy Residency Program
      </li>
      <li>
       Continuing Professional Development
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      The University of Minnesota’s College of Pharmacy believes patient care is at the center of pharmacy practice. Their Doctor of Pharmacy (PharmD) Professional Program is therefore aimed at educating students to become generalist pharmacy practitioners. The dual degree programs include a PharmD/MBA Dual Degree Program and a PharmD/MPH Dual Degree Program.
     </p>
     <p>
      You can read more about the different programs
      <a href="https://gocialirx.com/">
       here
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <h3>
      3. University of California San Francisco (UCSF)
     </h3>
     <p>
      The
      <a href="https://gocialirx.com/">
       UCSF School of Pharmacy
      </a>
      offers various degree programs, as well as postdoctoral and international programs. There are even career-development and leadership courses available.
     </p>
     <p>
      UCSF School of Pharmacy’s degree programs include the following single and combined degree programs:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       PharmD
      </li>
      <li>
       PharmD/MSCR
      </li>
      <li>
       PharmD/PhD
      </li>
      <li>
       Master of Translational Medicine degree program
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      Their Ph.D. graduate degree programs include:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       Ph.D. in Bioengineering (BioE)
      </li>
      <li>
       Ph.D. in Biological and Medical Informatics (Bioinformatics pathway) (BI)
      </li>
      <li>
       Ph.D. in Biophysics (BP)
      </li>
      <li>
       Ph.D. in Chemistry and Chemical Biology (CCB)
      </li>
      <li>
       Ph.D. in Pharmaceutical Sciences &amp; Pharmacogenomics (PSPG)
      </li>
     </ul>
     <h3>
      4. University of Michigan – Ann Arbor
     </h3>
     <p>
      <a href="https://gocialirx.com/">
       The University of Michigan College of Pharmacy
      </a>
      offers a wide range of programs you can choose from. These include a PharmD program, a Ph.D. in Clinical Pharmacy, Ph.D. in Medical Chemistry, and many more.
     </p>
     <p>
      The University of Michigan also offers unique dual programs such as a Dual PharmD and MBA Program, and Dual PharmD and MPH Program.
     </p>
     <h3>
      5. University of Texas – Austin
     </h3>
     <p>
      You can enroll in a Pharm.D. Program, various specialized graduate study programs, and advanced professional training and research at the University of Texas College of Pharmacy.
     </p>
     <p>
      Areas of research this pharmaceutical college focuses on is:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       Chemical Biology &amp; Drug Discovery
      </li>
      <li>
       Environmental &amp; Developmental Mechanisms of Health and Disease
      </li>
      <li>
       Health Outcomes, Economics, and Equity Research
      </li>
      <li>
       Infectious Diseases
      </li>
      <li>
       Molecular Mechanisms of Cancer
      </li>
      <li>
       Neuropharmacology and Addiction
      </li>
      <li>
       Translational Research in Drug Delivery
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      To find out more details about the various programs the University of Texas College of Pharmacy offers, take a look at their website
      <a href="https://pharmacy.utexas.edu">
       here
      </a>
      .
     </p>
