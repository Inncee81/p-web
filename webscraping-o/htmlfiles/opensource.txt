         <div class="block block-block clearfix" id="block-block-15">
          <h2>
           To promote and protect open source software and communities...
          </h2>
          <div class="content">
           <p>
            For
            <a href="https://opensource.org/history">
             over 20 years
            </a>
            the Open Source Initiative (OSI) has worked to raise awareness and adoption of open source software, and build bridges between
            <a href="https://opensource.org/affiliates">
             open source communities
            </a>
            of practice. As a
            <a href="https://opensource.org/sponsors#sponOps">
             global non-profit
            </a>
            , the OSI champions software freedom in society through education, collaboration, and infrastructure, stewarding the
            <a href="https://opensource.org/osd">
             Open Source Definition
            </a>
            (OSD), and preventing abuse of the ideals and ethos inherent to the open source movement.
           </p>
           <p>
            Open source software is made by
            <a href="https://opensource.org/members">
             many people
            </a>
            and distributed under an
            <a href="https://opensource.org/licenses">
             OSD-compliant license
            </a>
            which grants all the rights to use, study, change, and share the software in modified and unmodified form. Software freedom is essential to enabling community development of open source software.
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
        </div>

                     <span style="font-size:11pt;font-family:Arial;color:#000000;background-color:transparent;font-weight:400;font-style:normal;font-variant:normal;text-decoration:none;vertical-align:baseline;white-space:pre;white-space:pre-wrap;">

                      </a>

                       OSI Affiliate project
                      </a>
                      that works to facilitate worldwide collaboration in the development of technology. The past year has been a particularly exciting one -- achieving some groundbreaking wins for open source in aerospace. ORI’s co-founder and CEO, Michelle Thompson took some time out of her busy schedule to talk with me about their recent regulatory initiatives.
                     </span>

                    </p>

                      MT: Open Research Institute's mission is to provide a friendly, safe, and accessible place to do open source research and development for amateur radio and beyond. We have been fully operational since March 2019 and have contributed technical and regulatory work central to the mission of the international amateur radio service. This work is useful outside of the amateur community because it allows a wide variety of organizations to use open source communications technology where they would otherwise have to reinvent a wheel, or restrict the work to US persons only.
