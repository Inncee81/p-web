           <th style="padding:2px;">
            <h2 id="mp-tfa-h2" style="margin:3px; background:#e9caef; font-size:120%; font-weight:bold; border:1px solid #a3bfb1; text-align:left; color:#000; padding:0.2em 0.4em;">
             <span class="mw-headline" id="Bitcoin">
              Bitcoin
             </span>
            </h2>
           </th>
          </tr>
          <tr>
           <td style="color:#000;">
            <div id="mp-tfa" style="padding:2px 5px">
             <div class="floatleft">
              <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:BC_Logo_.png" title="Bitcoin usage worldwide.">
               <img alt="Bitcoin usage worldwide." height="64" src="/w/images/en/2/29/BC_Logo_.png" width="64"/>
              </a>
             </div>
             <p>
              <b>
               <a href="/wiki/Bitcoin" title="Bitcoin">
                Bitcoin
               </a>
              </b>
              is a decentralized
              <a href="/wiki/Digital_currency" title="Digital currency">
               digital currency
              </a>
              that enables instant payments to anyone, anywhere in the world. Bitcoin uses peer-to-peer technology to operate with no central authority:
              <a href="/wiki/Transaction" title="Transaction">
               transaction management
              </a>
              and
              <a href="/wiki/Controlled_supply" title="Controlled supply">
               money issuance
              </a>
              are carried out collectively by the network.
             </p>
             <p>
              The original Bitcoin software by
              <a href="/wiki/Satoshi_Nakamoto" title="Satoshi Nakamoto">
               Satoshi Nakamoto
              </a>
              was released under the MIT license.
Most client software, derived or "from scratch", also use open source licensing.
             </p>
             <p>
              Bitcoin is the first successful implementation of a
              <i>
               distributed crypto-currency
              </i>
              , described in part in 1998 by
              <a href="/wiki/Wei_Dai" title="Wei Dai">
               Wei Dai
              </a>
              on the cypherpunks mailing list. Building upon the notion that money is any object, or any sort of record, accepted as payment for goods and services and repayment of debts in a given country or socio-economic context, Bitcoin is designed around the idea of using cryptography to control the creation and transfer of money, rather than relying on central authorities.
             </p>
             <p>
              Bitcoins have all the desirable properties of a money-like good. They are portable, durable, divisible, recognizable, fungible, scarce and difficult to counterfeit.
             </p>
            </div>
           </td>
          </tr>
          <tr>
           <th style="padding:2px">
            <h2 id="mp-dyk-h2" style="margin:3px; background:#e9caef; font-size:120%; font-weight:bold; border:1px solid #a3bfb1; text-align:left; color:#000; padding:0.2em 0.4em;">
             <span class="mw-headline" id="Why.3F">
              Why?
             </span>
            </h2>
           </th>
          </tr>
          <tr>
           <td style="color:#000;padding:2px 5px 5px">
            <div id="mp-dyk">
             Bitcoin is P2P electronic cash that is valuable over legacy systems because of the monetary autonomy it brings to its users. Bitcoin seeks to address the root problem with conventional currency: all the trust that's required to make it work -- Not that justified trust is a bad thing, but trust makes systems brittle, opaque, and costly to operate. Trust failures result in systemic collapses, trust curation creates inequality and monopoly lock-in, and naturally arising trust choke-points can be abused to deny access to due process. Through the use of cryptographic proof, decentralized networks and open source software Bitcoin minimizes and replaces these trust costs.
             <ul>
              <li>
               Bitcoin
               <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Transactions" title="Transactions">
                Transactions
               </a>
               are:
               <ul>
                <li>
                 <b>
                  Permissionless
                 </b>
                 and
                 <b>
                  borderless
                 </b>
                 . The software can be installed by anybody worldwide.
                </li>
                <li>
                 <b>
                  Anonymous
                 </b>
                 . Bitcoin does not require any ID to use making it suitable for the unbanked, the privacy-conscious, computers or people in areas with underdeveloped financial infrastructure.
                </li>
                <li>
                 <b>
                  Private
                 </b>
                 . When used with care bitcoin can support
                 <a href="/wiki/Privacy" title="Privacy">
                  strong financial privacy
                 </a>
                 .
                </li>
                <li>
                 <b>
                  Censorship-resistant
                 </b>
                 . Nobody is able to block or freeze a transaction of any amount.
                </li>
                <li>
                 <b>
                  Fast
                 </b>
                 . Transactions can be made almost as fast as data can travel over the Internet.
                </li>
                <li>
                 <b>
                  Cheap
                 </b>
                 . Fees can be
                 <a href="/wiki/Lightning_Network" title="Lightning Network">
                  very very low
                 </a>
                 .
                </li>
                <li>
                 <a href="/wiki/Irreversible_Transactions" title="Irreversible Transactions">
                  <b>
                   Irreversible
                  </b>
                 </a>
                 once settled, like cash. (but
                 <a href="/wiki/Myths#Bitcoin_has_no_built-in_chargeback_mechanism_and_this_is_bad" title="Myths">
                  consumer protection is still possible
                 </a>
                 .)
                </li>
                <li>
                 Online and available
                 <b>
                  24 hours a day, 365 days per year
                 </b>
                 .
                </li>
               </ul>
              </li>
             </ul>
             <p>
              Bitcoin can also be a
              <a href="/wiki/Bitcoin_as_an_investment" title="Bitcoin as an investment">
               store of value
              </a>
              , some have said it is a "swiss bank account in your pocket".
             </p>
             <ul>
              <li>
               Stored Bitcoins:
               <ul>
                <li>
                 Cannot be printed or debased.
                 <b>
                  Only
                  <a href="/wiki/Controlled_supply" title="Controlled supply">
                   21 million bitcoins
                  </a>
                  will ever exist
                 </b>
                 .
                </li>
                <li>
                 Have
                 <b>
                  no storage costs
                 </b>
                 . They take up no physical space regardless of amount.
                </li>
                <li>
                 Are
                 <b>
                  easy to protect and hide
                 </b>
                 . Can be stored on a phone, computer, encrypted on a
                 <a href="/wiki/Storing_bitcoins" title="Storing bitcoins">
                  paper backup
                 </a>
                 or
                 <a href="/wiki/Brainwallet" title="Brainwallet">
                  memorized in your head
                 </a>
                 .
                </li>
                <li>
                 <b>
                  No counterparty risk
                 </b>
                 . If you keep the
                 <a href="/wiki/Private_key" title="Private key">
                  private key
                 </a>
                 of a bitcoin secret and the transaction has enough
                 <a href="/wiki/Confirmation" title="Confirmation">
                  confirmations
                 </a>
                 , then nobody can take them from you no matter for what reason, no matter how good the excuse, no matter what.
                </li>
                <li>
                 Can be under
                 <b>
                  divided possession
                 </b>
                 with
                 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Multisignature" title="Multisignature">
                  Multisignature
                 </a>
                 . For example with a 2-of-3 multisig scheme there would be
                 <i>
                  three
                 </i>
                 private keys, of which
                 <i>
                  any two
                 </i>
                 is enough to spend the money. Those three keys can be spread anywhere, perhaps in multiple locations or known by multiple people. No other asset does this, for example you cannot hold gold coins under multisig.
                </li>
               </ul>
              </li>
             </ul>
            </div>

           <td style="color:#000;padding:2px 5px 5px">
            <div id="mp-otd">
             <b>
              Q. What is Bitcoin?
             </b>
             <p>
              A. Bitcoin is a peer-to-peer currency. Peer-to-peer means that no central authority issues new money or tracks transactions. These tasks are managed collectively by the
              <a href="/wiki/Network" title="Network">
               network
              </a>
              .
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               Q. How does Bitcoin work?
              </b>
             </p>
             <p>
              A. Bitcoin uses
              <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public-key_cryptography" rel="nofollow">
               public-key cryptography
              </a>
              , peer-to-peer networking, and
              <a href="/wiki/Proof_of_work" title="Proof of work">
               proof-of-work
              </a>
              to process and verify payments. Bitcoins are sent (or signed over) from one address to another with each user potentially having many, many addresses. Each payment transaction is broadcast to the network and included in the blockchain so that the included bitcoins cannot be spent twice. After an hour or two, each transaction is locked in time by the massive amount of processing power that continues to extend the blockchain. Using these techniques, Bitcoin provides a fast and extremely reliable payment network that anyone can use.
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               Q. How do I edit this wiki?
              </b>
             </p>
             <p>
              A. Create a wiki account and
              <a href="/wiki/Bitcoin_Wiki:Editing_privileges" title="Bitcoin Wiki:Editing privileges">
               get it activated
              </a>
              .
             </p>
