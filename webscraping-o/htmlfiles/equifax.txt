                    <div class="efx-accordion__heading panel-heading" id="heading3498094-1" role="tab">
                     <h4 class="panel-title">
                      <a aria-controls="collapseExpandable3498094-1" class="collapsed" data-parent="#accordion3498094" data-toggle="collapse" href="#collapseExpandable3498094-1" role="button">
                       <strong>
                        <span style="color: #ea002c; margin-right: 5px;">
                         Q.
                        </span>
                        How do I place, temporarily lift, or permanently remove a security freeze?
                       </strong>
                      </a>
                     </h4>
                    </div>
                    <div class="efx-accordion__body panel-collapse collapse" data-parent="#accordion3498094" id="collapseExpandable3498094-1" role="tabpanel">
                     <div class="panel-body">
                      <p>
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       Placing, lifting and removing a security freeze is free. You can place, temporarily lift, or permanently remove a security freeze on your Equifax credit report in several ways.
                       <br/>
                      </p>
                      <ul data-aura-rendered-by="7:120;a">
                       <li>
                        <b>
                         Online
                        </b>
                        , by creating a
                        <a href="https://my.equifax.com/consumer-registration/rest/1.0/redirectPartnerTenant?intent=SECURITY_FREEZE" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
                         myEquifax
                        </a>
                        account. You can check the status of your security freeze through your myEquifax account as well.
                       </li>
                       <li>
                        <b>
                         By phone.
                        </b>
                        Call us at (888) 298-0045. You’ll be required to give certain information to verify your identity. You'll also have the option to receive a one-time PIN by text message or answer questions based on information in your Equifax credit report for identity verification.
                       </li>
                       <li>
                        <b>
                         By mail.
                        </b>
                        Please download
                        <a href="https://assets.equifax.com/assets/personal/Security_Freeze_Request_Form.pdf" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
                         this form
                        </a>
                        for instructions and mailing address. If you are requesting to temporarily lift or permanently remove a security freeze via mail, you’ll need to provide documents to validate your identity and address.
                        <a href="https://help.equifax.com/s/article/What-documentation-should-I-send-in-to-validate-my-ID-or-address" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
                         Read more about acceptable documents here
                        </a>
                        . After we receive the request and verify your identity, you will receive confirmation.
                       </li>
                      </ul>
                      <p>
                       <br/>
                       To place, temporarily lift or permanently remove a security freeze on your Experian or TransUnion credit reports, please contact them directly.
                       <br/>
                       <br/>
                       Experian:
                       <a href="https://www.experian.com/freeze/center.html" rel="nofollow,noopener" target="_blank">
                        https://www.experian.com/freeze/center.html
                       </a>
                       <br/>
                       TransUnion:
                       <a href="https://www.transunion.com/credit-freeze" rel="nofollow,noopener" target="_blank">
                        https://www.transunion.com/credit-freeze
                       </a>
                       <br/>
                       <br/>
                       To learn more about the difference between a security freeze and a credit report lock,
                       <a data-aura-rendered-by="7:120;a" href="https://help.equifax.com/s/article/What-s-the-difference-between-a-credit-report-lock-and-a-security-freeze" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
                        click here
                       </a>
                       .

                      <a aria-controls="collapseExpandable3498094-2" class="collapsed" data-parent="#accordion3498094" data-toggle="collapse" href="#collapseExpandable3498094-2" role="button">
                       <strong>
                        <span style="color: #ea002c; margin-right: 5px;">
                         Q.
                        </span>
                        How do I place a fraud alert or active duty alert?
                       </strong>
                      </a>
                     </h4>
                    </div>
                    <div class="efx-accordion__body panel-collapse collapse" data-parent="#accordion3498094" id="collapseExpandable3498094-2" role="tabpanel">
                     <div class="panel-body">
                      <p>
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       Placing a fraud alert on your credit reports is free.
                       <a href="https://my.equifax.com/consumer-registration/rest/1.0/redirectPartnerTenant?intent=FRAUD_ALERT" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
                        Click here
                       </a>
                       to place an initial one-year fraud alert or active duty alert on your Equifax credit report online by creating a myEquifax account. Once your fraud alert or active duty alert is placed, you can also check its status through myEquifax.
                       <br/>
                       <br/>
                       You can contact any of the three nationwide credit bureaus to request an initial fraud alert or active duty alert. Once you have placed an initial fraud alert or active duty alert on your credit report with one of the bureaus, that bureau will send a request to the other two bureaus to do the same, so you do not have to contact all three.
                       <br/>
                       <br/>
                       You can also request an initial one-year fraud alert or active duty alert:
                      </p>
                      <ul>
                       <li>
                        By calling: 1-800-525-6285; or
                       </li>
                       <li>
                        By downloading this
                        <a href="https://assets.equifax.com/assets/personal/Fraud_Alert_Request_Form.pdf" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
                         form
                        </a>
                        for an initial fraud alert or an active duty alert and mailing it or submitting a written request to:
                       </li>
                      </ul>
                      <p>
                       <br/>
                       Equifax Information Services LLC
                       <br/>
                       P.O. Box 105069
                       <br/>
                       Atlanta, GA 30348-5069
                       <br/>
                       <br/>
                       If you have been a victim of identity theft and have a police and/or identity theft report (available at
                       <a href="https://www.identitytheft.gov/" rel="nofollow,noopener" target="_blank">
                        www.identitytheft.gov
                       </a>
                       ), you have the right to request a seven-year extended fraud alert on your credit reports.  To submit your request, please download and follow the instructions on the
                       <a href="https://assets.equifax.com/assets/personal/Extended_Fraud_Alert_Request_Form.pdf" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
                        Extended Fraud Alert form
                       </a>
                       .
                      </p>

                    <div class="efx-accordion__heading panel-heading" id="heading3498094-3" role="tab">
                     <h4 class="panel-title">
                      <a aria-controls="collapseExpandable3498094-3" class="collapsed" data-parent="#accordion3498094" data-toggle="collapse" href="#collapseExpandable3498094-3" role="button">
                       <strong>
                        <span style="color: #ea002c; margin-right: 5px;">
                         Q.
                        </span>
                        How do I correct or dispute information on my credit reports?
                       </strong>
                      </a>
                     </h4>
                    </div>
                    <div class="efx-accordion__body panel-collapse collapse" data-parent="#accordion3498094" id="collapseExpandable3498094-3" role="tabpanel">
                     <div class="panel-body">
                      <p>
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       If you see information on your credit reports you believe is incomplete or inaccurate, a good first step is to contact the lender or creditor directly. This is especially helpful if the information involves your name or address. Updating your personal information with lenders and creditors can help ensure the information reported to the three nationwide credit bureaus—Equifax, Experian and TransUnion – is correct.
                       <br/>
                       <br/>
                       If you need to dispute information with the credit bureaus, the fastest way to do so is online. You’ll need to file a dispute separately with each credit bureau.
                       <br/>
                       <br/>
                       You can file a dispute on the following sites:
                      </p>
                      <ul>
                       <li>
                        Equifax:
                        <a href="https://myequifax.com/consumer-registration/rest/1.0/redirectPartnerTenant?intent=DISPUTE" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
                         www.myequifax.com
                        </a>
                        . New users will first need to create an account. Existing users can log in using their current credentials.
                       </li>
                       <li>
                        Experian:
                        <a href="https://www.experian.com/" rel="nofollow,noopener" target="_blank">
                         www.experian.com
                        </a>
                       </li>
                       <li>
                        TransUnion:
                        <a href="https://www.transunion.com/" rel="nofollow,noopener" target="_blank">
                         www.transunion.com
                        </a>
                       </li>
                      </ul>
                      <p>
                       To assist with your dispute, you may need to upload documents.  Please
                       <a href="https://help.equifax.com/s/article/What-information-do-I-need-to-provide-when-submitting-a-dispute" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
                        click here
                       </a>
                       to review documents that may be helpful and, at times, required.
                       <br/>
                       <br/>
                       Most dispute investigations are completed within 30 days, and you will receive a notification once your investigation is complete.
                       <br/>
                       <br/>
                       <a href="https://help.equifax.com/s/article/How-do-I-correct-or-dispute-inaccuracies-on-my-credit-file-by-mail" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
                        Click here
                       </a>
                       if you would prefer to dispute information via mail or phone.
                       <br/>

                       <a href="https://www.equifax.com/personal/credit-report-services" rel="noopener" target="_blank">

                       </a>

                        <span style="color: #ea002c; margin-right: 5px;">
                         Q.
                        </span>
                        What is a fraud alert?
                       </strong>
                      </a>
                     </h4>
                    </div>
                    <div class="efx-accordion__body panel-collapse collapse" data-parent="#accordion3498094" id="collapseExpandable3498094-6" role="tabpanel">
                     <div class="panel-body">
                      <p>
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       A fraud alert is a notice on your credit report that alerts creditors you may be a victim of fraud, including identity theft. A fraud alert can make it harder for someone to open unauthorized accounts in your name. It encourages lenders and creditors to take extra steps to verify your identity, such as contacting you by phone, before opening a new credit account in your name or making changes to existing accounts.
                       <br/>
                       <br/>
                       There are two types of fraud alerts you can place on your credit report, and both are free.
                       <br/>
                       <br/>
                       •
                       <b>
                        Initial fraud alert:
                       </b>
                       If you believe you are or may become a victim of fraud or identity theft, you may place an initial fraud alert on your credit report. An initial fraud alert remains on your credit report for one year, unless you choose to remove it sooner, and can be renewed for additional one-year periods.
                       <br/>
                       •
                       <b>
                        Extended fraud alert:
                       </b>
                       If you have a police report or a Federal Trade Commission Identity Theft Report showing you’re a victim of identity theft, you may place an extended fraud alert on your credit report. An extended fraud alert remains on your credit report for seven years unless you choose to remove it sooner.
                       <a href="https://assets.equifax.com/assets/personal/Extended_Fraud_Alert_Request_Form.pdf" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
                        Click here
                       </a>
                       for the extended fraud alert form.
                       <br/>
                       <br/>
                       You can contact any of the three nationwide credit bureaus to request a fraud alert. Once you have you placed a fraud alert on your credit report with one of the bureaus, that bureau will send a request to the other two bureaus to do the same, so you do not have to contact all three.
                       <br/>
                       <br/>
                       To place a fraud alert on your Equifax credit report,
                       <a href="https://www.equifax.com/personal/credit-report-services" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
                        click here
                       </a>
                       .
                       <br/>
                       <br/>

                       <a href="https://help.equifax.com/s/article/What-s-the-difference-between-a-fraud-alert-and-a-credit-lock-or-security-freeze" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
                        click here
