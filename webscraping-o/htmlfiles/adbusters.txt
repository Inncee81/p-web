     <h1 class="heading-53">
      #MakeSecrectTaboo
     </h1>
     <p class="p_flow2_1">
      Democracy is the defining virtue of Western political systems. We worry that if it fails we’ve got nothing.
     </p>
     <p class="p_flow2_2">
      That’s why what’s happening now is so troubling. Democracy seems to be on the verge of failing.
     </p>
     <p class="p_flow2_2">
      But the real glue in the gears of democracy isn’t what you think.
     </p>
     <p class="p_flow2_2">
      <strong>
       It’s
       <em>
        secrecy.
       </em>
      </strong>
     </p>
     <img alt="" loading="lazy" src="https://uploads-ssl.webflow.com/5ab163de19104964ce8a64b9/602d8da2b6c25c77607cf6d8_secrecy_taboo_report_450x559.jpg"/>
     <p class="p_flow2_2">
      Secrecy has become so commonplace that what should have been jaw-dropping geopolitical developments have passed us by, unnoticed.
     </p>
     <p class="p_flow2_2">
      Many of the dictators and strongmen of the past century were secretly propped up by Western governments. Almost all genocides and government-afflicted atrocities were orchestrated in secret.
     </p>
     <p class="p_flow2_2">
      There are always “good reasons” cited for withholding information. But the real reason so many public and corporate proceedings are classified isn’t for safety or national security interests. It’s to shield the actors and institutions from embarrassment and accountability. To let them cover their butts.
      <br/>
     </p>
     <p class="p_flow2_2">
      Here’s the thing: it should not be up to some brave individual to step up and shine the light on systemic institutional violence. It’s the person who hides vital information who should be rotting in jail.
      <br/>
     </p>
     <img alt="" class="img_flow2_1" loading="lazy" src="https://uploads-ssl.webflow.com/5ab163de19104964ce8a64b9/6054e860b4c0440b28afe755_edward_snowden_660x338.gif"/>
     <p class="p_flow2_2">
      Only when We the People know what’s going on can a political system truly be called a democracy. Because
      <em>
       the right to freely express yourself depends first on the ability to make an informed judgment
      </em>
      . That’s what “power to the people” means.
      <br/>
     </p>
     <img alt="" class="img_flow2_1" loading="lazy" src="https://uploads-ssl.webflow.com/5ab163de19104964ce8a64b9/6054e9194c3250745bd93df0_world_government_direct_web_democracy_450x675.gif"/>
     <p class="p_flow2_2">
      It’s unconscionable that it’s the victims of secrets who feel shame. It should be the secret-keepers who feel shame. It should be embarrassing to keep secrets. We need to keep hollering this from the rooftops until
      <em>
       keeping secrets becomes a taboo.
      </em>
      <br/>
     </p>
     <p class="p_flow2_2">
      Let’s make that the rallying cry of a new movement . . .
      <br/>
     </p>
     <p class="p_flow2_2">
      . . .a movement to
      <strong>
       <em>
        #MakeSecrecyTaboo.
       </em>
      </strong>
      <br/>
     </p>
     <img alt="" class="img_flow2_1" loading="lazy" src="https://uploads-ssl.webflow.com/5ab163de19104964ce8a64b9/6054e9ddc0ecb6c0eeea547a_eliminate_tax_havens_question_450x121.jpg"/>
     <img alt="" class="image-190" loading="lazy" src="https://uploads-ssl.webflow.com/5ab163de19104964ce8a64b9/601c87062fe85c282a057ea6_dry-brush-stroke-13.png"/>

            Myanmar's genocidal military government
           </a>
           have in common — that is, besides a shared taste for blood and brutality?
           <br/>
          </p>
          <p>
           They all, at one point, have been clients of the notorious Montreal-based lobbyist (read: propagandist) — and former Israeli intelligence officer — Ari Ben-Menashe.
           <br/>
          </p>
          <p>
           In the wake of the coup d'état that overthrew the country's civilian government, Ben-Menashe and his Montreal-based firm Dickens &amp; Madson (Canada) Inc.

           </a>

          </p>

            at least 183 people
           </a>
           since the coup, which took place on February 1st. Ben-Menashe will apparently be
           <a href="https://www.reuters.com/article/us-myanmar-politics-lobbyist-usa-idUSKBN2B20AR" target="_blank">
            paid $2 million
           </a>
           .
           <br/>
          </p>
          <p>
           The Canadian government and the federal police service (which has investigated Ben-Menashe for
           <a href="https://www.theglobeandmail.com/world/article-myanmar-junta-hires-israeli-canadian-lobbyist-to-explain-coup/" target="_blank">
            previous dealings with bad actors
           </a>
           ) say they are aware of the contract. A spokeswoman for Canada's foreign-affairs ministry said that it may be criminal, because Canada has imposed sanctions on Ben-Menashe's dissent-stifling employer.
           <br/>
          </p>

           <a href="https://www.justiceformyanmar.org/" target="_blank">

           </a>
           . "Ari is not only attempting to whitewash the reputation of these killers but he will be doing so with funds stolen from the people of Myanmar."
           <br/>
          </p>
          <p>

His pockets are lined with blood; his livelihood founded on aiding and abetting heinous, even genocidal tyrants. He and his ilk are examples of some of the worst atrocity-justifying propagandists that liberal democracies allow.

           </a>

          </p>

            Let’s get into something personal right off the hop. Let's talk about

             resistance
            </em>
            .

           <p>
            Resistance is what people feel when they just can’t face what needs to be done. Something important is right in front of them, but it’s so hairy, so scary in its implications on their life, their cozy way of keeping on keeping on, that they can’t deal with it.
           </p>
           <p>
            Resistance grips us when our very soul recognizes just how much is on the line. Like right now.
           </p>

           </p>
