       <h2>
        <a href="https://www.prisonpolicy.org/reports/juryexclusion.html">
         Rigging the jury: How each state reduces jury diversity by excluding people with criminal records
        </a>
       </h2>
       <p class="clear">
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="https://www.prisonpolicy.org/reports/juryexclusion.html">
         <picture>
          <source srcset="https://static.prisonpolicy.org/images/reportthumbs/juries_reportcover_250w.webp" type="image/webp">
           <source srcset="https://static.prisonpolicy.org/images/reportthumbs/juries_reportcover_250w.png" type="image/png">
            <img alt="report thumbnail" class="right reportcover thumb250" height="324" src="https://static.prisonpolicy.org/images/reportthumbs/juries_reportcover_250w.png" style="margin-bottom:2em" width="250"/>
           </source>
          </source>
         </picture>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Our first-of-its-kind report shows how all 50 states reduce jury diversity by excluding some people with criminal records from serving. Our report includes an easy-to-read map and detailed table describing each state’s policies, and explains how excluding people with criminal records makes juries less diverse — which in turn makes criminal trials even less fair.
       </p>
