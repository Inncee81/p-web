          <a href="https://www.mobygames.com/game/grand-theft-auto-iv">

           Grand Theft Auto V
          </a>

         <div class="sideBarContent color3">
          <h3 class="featured-title">
           <a href="https://www.mobygames.com/game/sheet/gameId=9227/">
            Rise of Nations
           </a>
          </h3>
          <a class="featured-box" href="https://www.mobygames.com/game/sheet/gameId,9227">
           <img height="170" src="/images/covers/s/21462-rise-of-nations-windows-front-cover.jpg" width="120"/>
          </a>
          Rise of Nations is what you get when you cross the RTS mechanics of
          <a href="https://www.mobygames.com/game/age-of-empires">
           Age of Empires
          </a>
          with the scale and timeline of
          <a href="https://www.mobygames.com/game/sid-meiers-civilization">
           Sid Meier's Civilization
          </a>
          . In essence, you get through humanity's history in an hour-long match on average, but without it being tedious like in
          <a href="https://www.mobygames.com/game/empire-earth">
           Empire Earth
          </a>
          .
          <br/>
          <br/>
          Every age a civilization goes through has its place without filler, and is nicely dosed so it does not become too overwhelming with options. While it has no story campaign to speak of, the core gameplay keeps itself interesting through unit and civilization variety, randomized maps using several interesting templates, and a meta-map mode created when the concept was rather new in the genre.

           by

            Plokite_Wolf
