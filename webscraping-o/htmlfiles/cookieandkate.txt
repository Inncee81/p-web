          How to Make Cauliflower Rice
         </a>
        </h2>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content">

         </noscript>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Have you tried cauliflower rice?
         </strong>
         It’s a perfectly versatile, light side dish. I was skeptical of the concept at first, but now I’ll admit that it
         <em>
          can
         </em>
         be awesome. I’ll share all my tricks with you today.
        </p>
        <p>
         If you, like me, are craving lighter meals as the weather warms up, try serving cauliflower rice instead of white or
         <a href="https://cookieandkate.com/cilantro-lime-brown-rice-recipe/">
          brown rice
         </a>
         . It’s similar in texture, with a nice savory flavor, and it’s pure vegetables! I’ve been trying to fill half of my plate with veggies lately, so cauliflower rice is just the ticket.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="cauliflower florets" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-38284" data-lazy-sizes="(max-width: 480px) 90vw, (max-width: 865px) 80vw, 550px" data-lazy-src="https://cookieandkate.com/images/2021/03/cauliflower-florets.jpg" data-lazy-srcset="https://cookieandkate.com/images/2021/03/cauliflower-florets.jpg 1100w, https://cookieandkate.com/images/2021/03/cauliflower-florets-550x378.jpg 550w, https://cookieandkate.com/images/2021/03/cauliflower-florets-270x186.jpg 270w, https://cookieandkate.com/images/2021/03/cauliflower-florets-768x528.jpg 768w" height="756" src="data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%201100%20756'%3E%3C/svg%3E" width="1100"/>
         <noscript>
          <img alt="cauliflower florets" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-38284" height="756" sizes="(max-width: 480px) 90vw, (max-width: 865px) 80vw, 550px" src="https://cookieandkate.com/images/2021/03/cauliflower-florets.jpg" srcset="https://cookieandkate.com/images/2021/03/cauliflower-florets.jpg 1100w, https://cookieandkate.com/images/2021/03/cauliflower-florets-550x378.jpg 550w, https://cookieandkate.com/images/2021/03/cauliflower-florets-270x186.jpg 270w, https://cookieandkate.com/images/2021/03/cauliflower-florets-768x528.jpg 768w" width="1100"/>
         </noscript>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          There is an art to cauliflower rice.
         </strong>
         If you haven’t enjoyed it in the past, it probably wasn’t prepared optimally. In fact, I just ordered the new cauliflower rice option at my favorite chain burrito place (you probably know it), and I was  disappointed. It was far too salty and way too watery. It can be so much better.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Here is a basic primer on how to prepare cauliflower rice.
         </strong>
         This recipe is simple and yields perfectly fluffy, lightly seared cauliflower rice. You’ll also learn how to adjust the recipe to suit many different meals. Off we go!
        </p>

       <header class="entry-header">
        <h2 class="entry-title">
         <a class="entry-title-link" href="https://cookieandkate.com/vegetarian-tortilla-soup/" rel="bookmark">
          Tortilla Soup
         </a>
        </h2>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time">
          March 3, 2021
         </time>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="https://cookieandkate.com/vegetarian-tortilla-soup/#comments">
           278 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="tortilla soup recipe" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-38127" data-lazy-sizes="(max-width: 480px) 90vw, (max-width: 865px) 80vw, 550px" data-lazy-src="https://cookieandkate.com/images/2013/11/tortilla-soup-recipe.jpg" data-lazy-srcset="https://cookieandkate.com/images/2013/11/tortilla-soup-recipe.jpg 1456w, https://cookieandkate.com/images/2013/11/tortilla-soup-recipe-548x824.jpg 548w, https://cookieandkate.com/images/2013/11/tortilla-soup-recipe-1098x1650.jpg 1098w, https://cookieandkate.com/images/2013/11/tortilla-soup-recipe-1022x1536.jpg 1022w, https://cookieandkate.com/images/2013/11/tortilla-soup-recipe-270x406.jpg 270w, https://cookieandkate.com/images/2013/11/tortilla-soup-recipe-768x1154.jpg 768w" height="2188" src="data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%201456%202188'%3E%3C/svg%3E" width="1456"/>
         <noscript>
          <img alt="tortilla soup recipe" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-38127" height="2188" sizes="(max-width: 480px) 90vw, (max-width: 865px) 80vw, 550px" src="https://cookieandkate.com/images/2013/11/tortilla-soup-recipe.jpg" srcset="https://cookieandkate.com/images/2013/11/tortilla-soup-recipe.jpg 1456w, https://cookieandkate.com/images/2013/11/tortilla-soup-recipe-548x824.jpg 548w, https://cookieandkate.com/images/2013/11/tortilla-soup-recipe-1098x1650.jpg 1098w, https://cookieandkate.com/images/2013/11/tortilla-soup-recipe-1022x1536.jpg 1022w, https://cookieandkate.com/images/2013/11/tortilla-soup-recipe-270x406.jpg 270w, https://cookieandkate.com/images/2013/11/tortilla-soup-recipe-768x1154.jpg 768w" width="1456"/>
         </noscript>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Do you enjoy tortilla soup? It’s one of my all-time favorites!
         </strong>
         Tortilla soup is fresh and flavorful, earthy and a little spicy. Topped with a big handful of crispy tortilla strips, it’s a light but satisfying meal for lunch or dinner. As a bonus, it tastes even better the next day.
        </p>
        <p>
         Tex-Mex tortilla soups most often include shredded chicken, but it’s not unusual to find vegetarian tortilla soups in Mexico. I can’t call this recipe fully authentic, though. It’s a delicious variation on classic tortilla soup (Sopa Azteca) that you can make at home any time you get the craving.
        </p>
        <p>
         In Mexico, you’ll find tons of regional variations and personal preferences for tortilla soup. For this recipe, I added black beans for some heft, as well as a red bell pepper because I love the flavor and texture it provides. I tried to keep the remaining ingredients in line with what you might find in Mexico, but to be honest, I have more personal experience with the Tex-Mex variations I’ve sampled over the years.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="tortilla soup ingredients" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-38132" data-lazy-sizes="(max-width: 480px) 90vw, (max-width: 865px) 80vw, 550px" data-lazy-src="https://cookieandkate.com/images/2013/11/tortilla-soup-ingredients.jpg" data-lazy-srcset="https://cookieandkate.com/images/2013/11/tortilla-soup-ingredients.jpg 1100w, https://cookieandkate.com/images/2013/11/tortilla-soup-ingredients-550x378.jpg 550w, https://cookieandkate.com/images/2013/11/tortilla-soup-ingredients-270x186.jpg 270w, https://cookieandkate.com/images/2013/11/tortilla-soup-ingredients-768x528.jpg 768w" height="756" src="data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%201100%20756'%3E%3C/svg%3E" width="1100"/>
         <noscript>
          <img alt="tortilla soup ingredients" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-38132" height="756" sizes="(max-width: 480px) 90vw, (max-width: 865px) 80vw, 550px" src="https://cookieandkate.com/images/2013/11/tortilla-soup-ingredients.jpg" srcset="https://cookieandkate.com/images/2013/11/tortilla-soup-ingredients.jpg 1100w, https://cookieandkate.com/images/2013/11/tortilla-soup-ingredients-550x378.jpg 550w, https://cookieandkate.com/images/2013/11/tortilla-soup-ingredients-270x186.jpg 270w, https://cookieandkate.com/images/2013/11/tortilla-soup-ingredients-768x528.jpg 768w" width="1100"/>
         </noscript>
        </p>
        <p>
         This recipe is a fully reworked version of an old tortilla soup recipe on the blog. Perhaps you’ve made that one and enjoyed it, but I tried it again recently and just knew I could build in more flavor along the way. If you want a copy of the original, I’ve saved a
         <a href="https://cookieandkate.com/images/2021/03/original-vegetarian-tortilla-soup-cookie-and-kate-4.pdf" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
          PDF of the recipe
         </a>
         right here.
        </p>
        <p>
         This soup is closer to the spin on tortilla soup in my cookbook,
         <a href="https://rstyle.me/+j50ZBuiDfVBAlAppgHOr_g" rel="nofollow noopener" target="_blank">
          Love Real Food
         </a>
         , which features an irresistible sweet-and-spicy interplay between sweet potatoes and jalapeño. Give that one a try, too!
        </p>

        <p>
         <strong>
          Can you feel it?
         </strong>
         Spring is coming! We made it to March. Asparagus is on its way and we’ll finally have more fresh fruits and vegetables to work with this month. I’m excited to grow even more tomatoes and veggies this year. Our new house came with several raised beds, so I’d better start planning.
        </p>
        <p>
         I’m working on colorful spring recipes for you, and playing with some interesting new cooking techniques. We’ll see which ones are worth sharing! For now, you can browse in-season recipes below.
        </p>
        <h2 class="seasonal">
         Asparagus
        </h2>

        </p>

        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          These roasted beets are for everyone!
         </strong>
         If you already love beetroots, I think you’ll be a big fan of this
         <em>
          foil-free, no-peeling-required
         </em>
         roasted beet recipe. If you don’t enjoy beets (yet), this roasting method just might change your mind.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          These roasted beets feature lightly caramelized, tender-but-not-quite-crisp exteriors and deep, condensed flavor.
         </strong>
         I don’t love beets in all applications, but these? I have to stop myself from devouring the whole pan before dinner.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="fresh beets" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-38087" data-lazy-sizes="(max-width: 480px) 90vw, (max-width: 865px) 80vw, 550px" data-lazy-src="https://cookieandkate.com/images/2021/02/fresh-beets.jpg" data-lazy-srcset="https://cookieandkate.com/images/2021/02/fresh-beets.jpg 1100w, https://cookieandkate.com/images/2021/02/fresh-beets-550x378.jpg 550w, https://cookieandkate.com/images/2021/02/fresh-beets-270x186.jpg 270w, https://cookieandkate.com/images/2021/02/fresh-beets-768x528.jpg 768w" height="756" src="data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%201100%20756'%3E%3C/svg%3E" width="1100"/>
         <noscript>
          <img alt="fresh beets" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-38087" height="756" sizes="(max-width: 480px) 90vw, (max-width: 865px) 80vw, 550px" src="https://cookieandkate.com/images/2021/02/fresh-beets.jpg" srcset="https://cookieandkate.com/images/2021/02/fresh-beets.jpg 1100w, https://cookieandkate.com/images/2021/02/fresh-beets-550x378.jpg 550w, https://cookieandkate.com/images/2021/02/fresh-beets-270x186.jpg 270w, https://cookieandkate.com/images/2021/02/fresh-beets-768x528.jpg 768w" width="1100"/>
         </noscript>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          With this method, simply scrub the beets well before baking.
         </strong>
         No need to peel before
         <em>
          or
         </em>
         after baking. The skin, which is perfectly edible, just seems to disappear during the baking process.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          The trick to pan-roasted beets is to drop the temperature a bit.
         </strong>
         I always roast
         <a href="https://cookieandkate.com/roasted-potatoes-recipe/">
          potatoes
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="https://cookieandkate.com/perfect-roasted-brussels-sprouts-recipe/">
          Brussels sprouts
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="https://cookieandkate.com/perfect-roasted-carrots-recipe/">
          carrots
         </a>
         , and pretty much all the other veggies at 425 degrees Fahrenheit.
        </p>
        <p>
         For beets, since we
         <em>
          don’t
         </em>
         want crispy outsides (tender is nicer), we’ll roast at 375 degrees. Easy!
        </p>
