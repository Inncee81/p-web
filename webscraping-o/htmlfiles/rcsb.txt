        <h2 class="visible-xs">
         Welcome
        </h2>
        <h3>
         A Structural View of Biology
        </h3>
        <p>
         This resource is powered by the Protein Data Bank archive-information about the 3D shapes of proteins, nucleic acids, and complex assemblies that helps students and researchers understand all aspects of biomedicine and agriculture, from protein synthesis to health and disease.
        </p>
        <p class="hidden-md hidden-sm">
         As a member of the wwPDB, the RCSB PDB curates and annotates PDB data.
        </p>
        <p class="hidden-sm">
         The RCSB PDB builds upon the data by creating tools and resources for research and education in molecular biology, structural biology, computational biology, and beyond.
        </p>

      <div class="panel_detailcontent hide" id="deposit_validate_panel">
       <img class="pull-right panel-single-image-longwidth hidden-xs" data-src="https://cdn.rcsb.org/rcsb-pdb/v2/home/images/panel_deposit/wwpdb_validationreports.png" src="data:image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAEAAAABCAQAAAC1HAwCAAAAC0lEQVR42mNkYAAAAAYAAjCB0C8AAAAASUVORK5CYII="/>
       <h3>
        Validation Services
       </h3>
       <p>
        Validation reports contain an assessment of the quality of a structure and highlight specific concerns by considering the coordinates of the model, the experimental data and the fit between the two. Easily interpretable summary information that compares the quality of a model with that of other models in the archive will help users of PDB data to critically assess archived entries and to select the most appropriate structural models for their needs. These reports are developed using the recommendations of the
        <a href="http://www.wwpdb.org/task/validation-task-forces.php" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
         wwPDB Validation Task Forces
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        Reports for released entries are available from Structure Summary pages.
       </p>
       <p>
        Validation reports for manuscript reviewers are created during annotation of deposited structures.
       </p>
       <p>
        Information and&amp;nbsp
        <a href="http://www.wwpdb.org/validation/validation-reports.php" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
         example Validation Reports (at wwpdb.org)
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="https://validate-rcsb.wwpdb.org" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
         Check
        </a>
        your X-ray, NMR, or EM structures before depositing (standalone server).
       </p>

       <h3>
        Structure Quality
       </h3>
       <p>
        Structure Summary pages provide access to information about structure quality.
       </p>
       <p>
        The slider graphic compares important global quality indicators for a given structure with the PDB archive. Global percentile ranks (black vertical boxes) are calculated with respect to all X-ray structures available prior to 2011. Resolution-specific percentile ranks (white vertical boxes) are calculated considering entries with similar resolution.
       </p>
       <p>
        This graphic is from the&amp;nbsp
        <a href="http://wwpdb.org/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
         wwPDB
        </a>
        &amp;nbsp
        <strong>
         Validation Report
        </strong>
        &amp;nbsp, which provides a more detailed assessment of the quality of a structure and highlights specific concerns. These reports were created using the recommendations of wwPDB Validation Task Forces.
        <span class="hidden-lg">
         The full wwPDB Validation Report PDF is available for download.&amp;nbsp
        </span>
        PDFs of&amp;nbsp
        <strong>
         Ramachandran plots
        </strong>
        (created by MolProbity) are provided to offer an independent method to evaluate the conformational quality of protein structures.
       </p>
       <p class="hidden-md hidden-sm">
        For examples, view the Structure Summary pages for&amp;nbsp
        <a href="/structure/1CBS">
         1CBS
        </a>
        &amp;nbsp(1.8Å structure of a small protein and a ligand&amp;nbsp
        <span class="hidden-lg">

        <a href="/structure/1FCC">
         1FCC

       <h3>
        EPPIC Biological Assemblies
       </h3>
       <p>
        <a href="http://eppic-web.org/ewui/" target="_blank">
         EPPIC (Evolutionary Protein-Protein Interface Classifier)
        </a>
        provides value-added information about biological assemblies in the PDB. This web server classifies interfaces present in protein crystals to distinguish biological interfaces from crystal contacts. EPPIC Version 3 enumerates all possible symmetric assemblies with a prediction of the most likely assembly based on probabilistic scores from pairwise evolutionary scoring.
       </p>

         available
