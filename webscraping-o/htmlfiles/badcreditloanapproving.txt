      <p>
       When you are carrying a low credit score and a higher lending credit risk, it is known as bad credit. In such cases, lenders commonly avoid handing over loans to such individuals who have a history of bad credit as the borrower might not be able to make the repayments on time.
      </p>
      <p>
       The credit history of the borrower is determined based on several factors like the amount borrowed, the amount of credit available, the number of times the borrower has credited on the repayment, etc. The credit score decreases whenever you are charged with a late repayment fee, you ask for more time for the loan repayment, or request a loan renewal.
      </p>
      <p>
       Mostly, the reason why some borrowers are unable to make timely loan repayments might because the person might be facing financial difficulty. Due to this, repaying the loan becomes difficult, and this leads to a bad credit score. Thankfully, this does not imply that the borrower will never be handed another loan until the previous one has been cleared.
      </p>
      <p>
       Even people with bad credit scores can borrow a loan; this loan is known as bad credit loans. However, it should be remembered that bad credit loans have a higher rate of interest, simply because of bad credit history. If you have a bad credit score and looking to borrow a loan, you need to check everything that is required for the loan; if your application gets rejected, your credit score falls lower than before.
      </p>
      <p>
       A bad credit loan is a loan for people who have a bad credit history. Some reasons why your credit history might be bad are:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        When you do not repay the loan on time
       </li>
       <li>
        Defaulting on the repayments
       </li>
       <li>
        A loan application is rejected
       </li>
       <li>
        You have never applied for a loan and lenders are unsure whether you would be able to make the repayments on time
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h2 class="js-animate anim-left">
       How Do Bad Credit Loans Function?
      </h2>
      <p>
       The workings of bad credit loans are as follows:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        You need to submit a request for the loan
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       You will need to get in touch with the lender. Even if you cannot do so directly, some parties act as intermediaries between you and the borrower. If you decide to go for the online loans for bad credit, all you need to do is first submit a simple form. These forms will ask you for necessary information like the type of credit, the requested amount, the reason for the loan, your name and contact details, etc.
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Receive offers from the lenders
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       Once you submit the form, the lender will collect and read through the provided information; alternatively, if you are applying for this loan via middlemen, these third-parties will look for lenders that can offer you the type of loan you are looking for.
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Review and accept the loan terms
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       Once you land the right loan lender, it is time to consider the terms and conditions of the loan. You will be offered loans by several lenders with different terms and rates of interest. However, it is up to you to make the ultimate decision. You need to look at the proposals put forward by the lender carefully and check whether it meets your circumstances or not. If it does not, you can freely turn down the offer and look for another lender.
      </p>
      <p>
       For instance, let us consider that you have a limited amount of income right now. Hence, you are looking for a loan that can be spread thin over a longer duration of time. This way, your monthly payments are kept in check. While this type of loan might attract a higher rate of interest, it could be worthwhile since you will have a lower monthly expense.
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Sign the contract and get the loan
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       After you have read all the terms and conditions and agreed to abide by them, you can finalize the loan. Be wary of the fact that until you sign the contract, you are under no obligation to the lender or financial institution; you have the option to walk away. If you have agreed to take the loan, the lender will finalize all the other details, which you will have to accept, and then receive the loan.
      </p>
      <h2 id="what-are-the-requirements-to-avail-bad-credit-loans">
       What Are The Requirements To Avail Bad Credit Loans?
      </h2>
      <p>
       Primarily, bad credit loans can fall into the two most basic types of loans  secured and unsecured loans. By definition, secured loans are the type of loans that are granted to you after you pledge an asset to the lender. This type of loan is beneficial for both the borrower and the lender  the borrower gets the loan at decent and flexible interest rates and tenure periods. In contrast, the lender becomes the temporary owner of the asset that the borrower has handed over as an assurance.
      </p>
      <p>
       If the borrower does not have any kind of collateral to pledge, then the bad credit loans turn into an unsecured loan. A particular loan is termed as an unsecured loan because the lender lends a loan without receiving any asset. This type of loan is mostly handed over by financial institutions and banks to individuals with high monthly incomes or working with reputable and well-known firms and companies. While you might not have to hand over an asset on this type of loan, the rates of interest are higher, and the loan amount might be lower as well.
      </p>
      <p>
       If you choose an unsecured bad credit loan, the lender will look into your credit history and check how often you were able to pay off your previous loans. Based on this information, the lender will get an idea of your loan history. Then the lender will ask for your proof of income, which he/she will use to determine if you will be able to repay the personal loans for bad credit within the time.
      </p>
      <p>
       In both types of loans, you will be asked to produce certain documents like:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Bank Default proof
       </li>
       <li>
        Signature verification proof
       </li>
       <li>
        Proof of age and identity
       </li>
       <li>
        Proof of residence
       </li>
       <li>
        Bank statements
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       In the case of business, audited balance sheets of the latest three years and/or company profile with an existing product range
      </p>
      <h3 id="why-can-you-be-denied-bad-credit-loans">
       Why Can You Be Denied Bad Credit Loans?
      </h3>
      <p>
       Several reasons why you can be rejected home loans for bad credit. Since most financial institutions and banks have their criteria for approving bad credit loans, the reason why your application gets denied might vary. Some of the most common causes include:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Multiple applications and inquiries for loan
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       Each time you apply for bad credit loans, the financial institution or bank will make an inquiry about your credit report and generate your credit score. These mentions and hard questions will be reflected on your credit report. If you apply for bad credit loans multiple times, this will portray you as a credit hungry person to financial institutions, lenders, and banks, which is unable to manage his/her finance and always looking to borrow money.
      </p>
      <p>
       Hence, multiple loan applications are one of the top reasons why your application for bad credit loans can get rejected.
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Error in the credit report and/or poor credit history
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       The overall credit score plays a vital role in determining whether the bad credit loans that you have applied for gets approved or rejected. The credit score needs to range between 300 and 900, and a credit score is allocated each time you make a credit repayment.
      </p>
      <p>
       The credit score also keeps tabs on the defaulting frequency. The credit score reflects the minimum and maximum possibility of default, which is also known as the probability of default. The more the likelihood of default percentage, the higher is the risk of defaulting on bad credit loans.
      </p>
      <p>
       If you find any error in your credit report, you need to raise the dispute with your credit bureau and resolve the matter as soon as possible. Incorrect credit reporting will lower your chances of availing bad credit loans.
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Higher existing debt
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       Bad credit loans might get rejected if you already have massive obligations from previous loans. If you already have a lot of open credit lines like loans and credit cards, the lender will think that you are already overburdened with credit and will likely reject your application for the loan. It should be understood that lenders will always look at high debts as risks.
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Unstable employment history
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       Most commonly, financial institutions and banks will offer almost all types of loans to people who have a stable employment history. If you do not have a steady job or are unable to hold one for more than a couple of months, it is more likely that your application for a loan will be rejected.
      </p>
      <p>
       Most financial institutions and banks prefer handing over bad credit loans to customers who have a regular income. If you keep changing your job frequently, you might just be able to avail of the loan; however, the interest rates will be much higher than what is typically issued.
      </p>
      <h2 id="what-is-the-cost-of-bad-credit-loans">
       What Is The Cost Of Bad Credit Loans?
      </h2>
      <p>
       Bad credit loans tend to be very costly. Apart from the borrowed amount, you need to pay back a lot more, like:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Higher rate of interest
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       Bad credit loans are the type of loans that will provide you with finances when you have no other option. As most of us already know, a bad credit score makes it highly challenging to apply for most other types of loans. But bad credit loans will charge you interest rates that might possibly be three-times higher than usual. Even in case you decide to pay off installment loans for bad credit, and you will irrevocably end up paying more than what is necessary.
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Higher insurance rate
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       The premium of the insurance policy is determined by the credit-based insurance score and done so that individuals do not file claims on the insurance to take out finance for improving their bad credit scores. While bad credit loans are not the only component that increases your insurance rate, it prevents you from applying for something at a lower rate. While it might depend from one insurance company to another, you will still leave out a lot of finances.
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Missed employment opportunities
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       If you have taken bad credit loans, it will mostly be shown on your credit score. If you work for the government or in the finance sector, you might need to submit a credit check. If you have bad credit, it would mean that you are compromised financially. Depending on the potential pay raise and career aspirations, this could reflect poorly on you. It is also the reason why most employers do not run a credit check when applying for a job in other sectors.
      </p>
      <h3 id="what-happens-if-you-are-unable-to-repay-bad-credit-loans">
       What Happens If You Are Unable To Repay Bad Credit Loans?
      </h3>
      <p>
       Some things that can happen if you are unable to repay your bad credit loans are:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Racking up the late fees
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       If you happen to miss out on your loan repayment before the due date, you will only be adding more money to what you already owe to the lender. While the late fee might depend on the lender, the precise amount can be found easily on the loan agreement. The late fee will become a part of the next installment.
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Damaging the credit score
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       If you have to apply for bad credit loans, this means that your credit score is already bad. However, defaulting on this loan will worsen your credit score and create havoc in your life. Even a small delay can create devastating ripples and damage your credit score.
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Having to go to the court
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       Another reason why you should always avoid defaulting on bad credit loans is that if the lender is not getting back at least a minimum amount of what you owe, there is a possibility that he/she will seek legal remedy. Mostly, you will be taken to court, and the judge will rule the case in favor of the lender. Additionally, while you are repaying the debt, you will also have to pay the court fees, thereby making the repayment job harder than before.
      </p>
      <h2 id="some-alternatives-of-bad-credit-loans-you-need-to-avoid">
       Some Alternatives Of Bad Credit Loans You Need To Avoid

      </ul>

However, the bank charges a fee for the overdraft protection. If you are already out of finance, adding a service fee does not help.
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Pawn Shop Loan
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       When you are looking for fast finances, you can exchange a valuable asset, like your computer or bike, at a pawnshop for bad credit loans. The pawn shop will then hand over a percentage of the items value and offer a short period to pay back the amount with interest. However, it should be noted that the rates of interest can reach as high as 120%. Hence, you must read the terms and conditions before agreeing to take the loan.
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Cash Advance Loan
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
