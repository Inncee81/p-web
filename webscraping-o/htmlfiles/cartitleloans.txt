        <h2 id="car-title-loans-whats-this">
         Car Title Loans: What’s This?
        </h2>
        <p>
         Car title loans, also known as title pledge or pawn, are one of the types of secured loans that are given for a short term. You mortgage your vehicle as collateral to a lender for this period until the loan is fully paid back. Normally, the repayment term lasts in 30 days.
        </p>
        <p>
         The reason why title loans are so alluring is because the lenders don’t pay regard to your credit history. It may be poor, but it will not point your actual financial position since your vehicle is already a direct demonstration. The application process usually lasts from 15 to 30 minutes. And the best thing of car title loans is that you keep on driving your vehicle.
        </p>
        <p>
         In order to get your loan approved, the vehicle must be clear and free. You will be given a loan that is equal to an estimated value of your vehicle. Bear in mind, if you don’t manage to pay the loan back, your vehicle is automatically comes into the lender’s possession.
        </p>
        <h2 id="how-do-car-title-loans-work">
         How Do Car Title Loans Work?
        </h2>
        <p>
         There are various forms of title loans that have various terms and conditions. You can submit an application either on-site or online. The main requirement is that you have to provide a lender with your sole car ownership, title, that must be clear.
        </p>
        <p>
         The lender determines the value of your vehicle (it can be a car or a motorcycle) and offers you a loan that is hinged on the vehicle’s value. Once your application is approved, this involves high interest rates. For instance, to borrow $1000, you will be charged 25% * that will be $1250. There might be some additional fee, including documentation, origination, and transaction fees and so on.
        </p>
        <p>
         Mostly, the lender will require information about your employment and some personal details. But in fact, it will not check it since your vehicle is a mortgage.
        </p>
        <p>
         Some lenders may ask you for additional keys or put a tracking system into your vehicle. That is the way they secure all guarantees.
        </p>
        <p>
         The lender will not approve your application if you have already got a loan from another lender. If your vehicle has already a lien, it means you are in debt, so the lender will refuse you even in a small loan.
        </p>

      <h2 class="js-animate anim-left">
       The Advantages Of Title Car Loans
      </h2>
      <p>
       In case of any financial troublesome situations, title loans are the best option for those who are in a cash need and own a vehicle, but have a non-perfect credit history. The lending company will not even ask a credit check. The lender will not ask you about the purpose of the loan, as it is your own problem. Since your mortgage is your vehicle, there is no point for lenders to check your financial and personal information. So, it all comes down to the instant approval of your loan.
