       <h2 class="entry-title">
        Foo’s home page
       </h2>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         <strong>
          <br/>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="https://bar.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/foo1.gif">
          <img alt="foo1" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4" height="426" loading="lazy" sizes="(max-width: 434px) 100vw, 434px" src="https://bar.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/foo1.gif" srcset="https://www.bar.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/foo1.gif 434w, https://www.bar.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/foo1-300x294.gif 300w" width="434"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Back in the earliest of early days, I (The Foo at bar.com) got a few emails a week, mostly from sysadmin type people who were invoking The Foo in an effort to debug some kind of system or other.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="https://bar.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/foo2.gif">
          <img alt="foo2" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-5" height="436" loading="lazy" sizes="(max-width: 434px) 100vw, 434px" src="https://bar.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/foo2.gif" srcset="https://www.bar.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/foo2.gif 434w, https://www.bar.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/foo2-150x150.gif 150w, https://www.bar.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/foo2-298x300.gif 298w" width="434"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Of course I, being a gregarious sort, answered the messages.  Mostly along the lines of “hello?  Foo here.  What can I do for you?” or “who you?  I Foo.”
        </p>
        <p>
         I met a lot of really interesting people in 1994 and 1995 that way.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="https://bar.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/foo3.gif">
          <img alt="foo3" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6" height="430" loading="lazy" sizes="(max-width: 434px) 100vw, 434px" src="https://bar.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/foo3.gif" srcset="https://www.bar.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/foo3.gif 434w, https://www.bar.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/foo3-150x150.gif 150w, https://www.bar.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/foo3-300x297.gif 300w" width="434"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         But soon I had to return to obscurity, as my email volume turned into craziness.
        </p>
        <p>
         Y’see people building web sites started putting little “give us your email address and we’ll let you see the goodies” challenges in their web sites, and lots of folks entered
         <a href="/cdn-cgi/l/email-protection#63050c0c230102114d000c0e">
          <span class="__cf_email__" data-cfemail="33555c5c735152411d505c5e">
           [email protected]
          </span>
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         Soon, I was getting thousands, then tens of thousands of emails a day, mostly from people who didn’t care whether I replied or not.   Alas, I was overwhelmed and had to return to my solitary life.
        </p>
        <p>
         For a while, I MX’d email addressed to me to 127.0.0.1 but that made some people cranky (although I still take some quiet pleasure at the thought of what that address did to spammers).
        </p>
        <p>
         I MX’d the mail over to a friend’s spam-detection system for about 4 hours one time, but the volume crashed his server and he asked for relief.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="https://bar.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/foo4.gif">
          <img alt="foo4" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-7" height="437" loading="lazy" sizes="(max-width: 434px) 100vw, 434px" src="https://bar.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/foo4.gif" srcset="https://www.bar.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/foo4.gif 434w, https://www.bar.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/foo4-150x150.gif 150w, https://www.bar.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/foo4-297x300.gif 297w" width="434"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         So now I’m content to tell you this small story.
        </p>
        <p>
         PS:  My friend
         <a href="http://www.haven2.com">
          Mike O’Connor
         </a>
         manages this domain for me.
        </p>
       </div>
