      <p>
       A number of utilities in the US Southeast want to create the Southeast Energy Exchange Market (SEEM). They filed a proposal to the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission (FERC) in February, and they want an approval by May 13. The nonprofit advocacy group the
       <a href="https://cleanenergy.org/" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">
        Southern Alliance for Clean Energy
       </a>
       (SACE) explained why SEEM isn’t a good move for the transition to green energy or reducing energy costs.
      </p>
