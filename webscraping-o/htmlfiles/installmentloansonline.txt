      <p>
       If you are nursing the thought of getting an installment loan, brush up on your finance knowledge. In this article, we'll discuss what an installment loan is and how the whole process works.
      </p>
      <h2 id="what-is-an-installment-loan">
       What is an Installment loan?
      </h2>
      <p>
       An installment loan is a loan that allows you to borrow money and payback in installments or fixed payments over some time or term. When referring to an Installment loan, the word 'term' means the time you have to repay a loan. For example, a 36-month term allows repayment over three years.
      </p>
      <h2 id="types-of-installment-loans">
       Types of Installment loans
      </h2>
      <p>
       Most of the popular loans people take out are categorized as Installment loans. Mortgages, student loans, personal loans, auto loans are all types of Installment loans. Below is a summary of the three popular types of  Installment loans :
      </p>
      <p>
       ·         Auto loans
      </p>
      <p>
       Auto loans, also known as car loans, are a common type of an installment loan offered by credit unions and banks. The interest rate for Auto loans typically falls between 3% and 15%
      </p>
      <p>
       ·         Personal loans
      </p>
      <p>
       Personal loans are another common type of installment loan. Credit unions and banks offer these loans. Although these loans can be used for any purpose, most people use it to consolidate debt.
      </p>
      <p>
       The interest rate for personal loans is generally between 6% to 36%, with terms between 2-5 years. Rates, conditions, and loan features for personal loans are different among lenders, so it's prudent to compare these loans from multiple lenders.
      </p>
      <p>
       ·         Mortgages
      </p>
      <p>
       The mortgage, also known as home loans, is arguably the most traditional installment loan. A mortgage is an Installment loan used to buy to a house. Typical mortgage terms are 15 to 30 years, with the option of an adjustable or fixed interest rate.
      </p>
      <h2 id="where-can-you-get-an-installment-loan">
       Where can you get an installment loan?
      </h2>
      <p>
       There are several numbers of places you can get an installment loan. However, in the digital era, everything starts with the internet. You can begin by typing installment loans near me in the search bar. That will reveal many options for you. Sof these places include:
      </p>
      <h2 id="banks">
       Banks
      </h2>
      <p>
       Banks are the number one source for different types of Installment loans, including mortgages, student loans, and auto loans. However, you will need to establish a relationship with any Bank of your choice by opening a current or savings account.
      </p>
      <h2 id="credit-unions">
       Credit Unions
      </h2>
      <p>
       Credit unions offer members based on the geographic region, the organization they work for, or others. Unlike banks, Credit unions offer lower rates and lower terms to people seeking installment loans.
      </p>
      <h2 id="online-lenders">
       Online lenders
      </h2>
      <p>
       Online installment loans are becoming increasingly popular nowadays and are now a prime source of Installment loans. Applying for an installment loan online is often fast and well streamlined.
      </p>
      <h2 id="how-do-you-qualify-for-an-installment-loan">
       How Do You Qualify for an Installment Loan?
      </h2>
      <p>
       When applying for an online installment loan or from any other source, there are some factors the borrower will use to evaluate your application.
      </p>
      <p>
       ·         Good credit score : Just like any different kind of loan, the better your credit score, the more your chances of getting approved increases. Generally, a FICO score of 700 is deemed suitable, while 800 above is considered excellent. Also, a higher credit score will offer you a lower interest rate.
      </p>
      <p>
       ·         Good credit history : Do you pay your debts on time? Is there any case of bankruptcies? Do you have any public blemishes on record? All these you need to consider before applying for an Installment loan.
      </p>
      <p>
       ·         Stable source of income : Every lender will verify if a borrower has a stable source of income. Checking this will ensure the borrower will be able to make all payments on time and in full.
      </p>
      <p>
       ·         Collateral : For some big or small installment loans, you must provide collateral to the lender. The guarantee provided will serve as a form of security.
