         <p class="cyhZ9">
          Earlier this week, the acting chair of the US Securities and Exchange Commission, Allison Lee, gave a
          <a href="https://www.sec.gov/news/speech/lee-climate-change">
           barnburner of a speech
          </a>
          . The Donald Trump appointee laid out a sweeping agenda promising to make ESG (environmental, social, and governance) issues central to the SEC’s mission, and potentially part of almost every financial transaction.
         </p>
         <p class="cyhZ9">
          “That supposed distinction—between what’s ‘good’ and what’s profitable, between what’s sustainable environmentally and what’s sustainable economically, between acting in pursuit of the public interest and acting to maximize the bottom line—is increasingly diminished,” Lee told the audience at the liberal think tank Center for American Progress. “[There’s] no historical precedent for the magnitude of the shift in investor focus that we’ve witnessed over the last decade.”
         </p>
         <p class="cyhZ9">
          If Lee’s vision succeeds—the SEC now opens a
          <a href="https://www.sec.gov/news/public-statement/lee-climate-change-disclosures">
           90-day comment period
          </a>
          —get ready for standardized reporting and mandatory climate disclosures, more risk reporting by private companies, detailed political spending disclosures related to ESG commitments, and a coordinated effort within the US government and internationally to integrate these initiatives into the world’s financial systems. It would mark a sharp acceleration of trends and a major break from the past: The SEC’s
          <a href="https://www.sec.gov/rules/interp/2010/33-9106.pdf">
           2010 guidelines
          </a>
          (pdf) on climate disclosure were loosely, if ever, followed.
         </p>
         <p class="cyhZ9">
          Recasting ESG issues as a “core risk” to investors sets the stage to force companies to track, account, and disclose them. But of course, the private sector is already there. BlackRock, the world’s largest asset manager, put Wall Street on notice last year, saying it plans to base all of its investment decisions on ESG criteria. “
          <a href="https://www.blackrock.com/corporate/investor-relations/larry-fink-ceo-letter">
           Climate risk is investment risk
          </a>
          ,” BlackRock chief Larry Fink wrote in his letter to CEOs. With more than
          <a href="https://corpgov.law.harvard.edu/2020/04/27/2020-proxy-season-preview/">
           half of all recent shareholder proposals
          </a>
          focused on environmental and social issues, executives can’t escape it.
         </p>
         <p class="cyhZ9">
          There are now two essential tracks: the central banks and the corporate sector. Both are moving relatively quickly in the same direction, toward a day when standardized ESG reporting will become mandatory and private-sector pressure will make ESG screens, especially climate change, part of every major investor’s financial calculus.
         </p>
         <p class="cyhZ9">
          “This is no longer a shouting match between divestment campaigners and banks that don’t want to do anything,” says Ilmi Granoff, director of sustainable finance at the ClimateWorks Foundation. “It’s now the federal regulator conducting serious oversight of a serious set of risks.” —
          <em>
           Michael J. Coren
          </em>
         </p>

           Daydreaming, flatulence, and chapped lips.
          </strong>

          <a href="https://qz.com/1984717/what-are-astrazenecas-self-reported-side-effects/">
           illustrating the illogical assumptions
          </a>
          made about the shot. Several European countries halted their administration of the vaccine after reports of blood clots, though the number of those who self-reported that response (1 in 167,000) essentially matches the prevalence of blood clots in the population. The drug company is diligently tracking all the bizarre “symptoms” experienced by Europeans anyway—the full list is guaranteed to make you chuckle.

The collection of photos—a contrast to those of empty landmarks a year ago—seems to
          <a href="https://qz.com/1985950/westminster-abbey-yankee-stadium-unique-places-to-get-a-covid-19-vaccine/">
