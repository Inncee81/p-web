      Samford University
     </h1>

       <h2 style="margin-top: .5em;">
        Academics
       </h2>
       <p>
        Samford is a leading Christian university offering undergraduate programs grounded in the liberal arts with an array of nationally recognized graduate and professional schools: arts, arts and sciences, business, divinity, education, health professions, law, nursing, pharmacy and public health.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="/programs/schools">
         Samford Academics
        </a>
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="fourcol">
       <img alt="Graduating Students in their caps and gowns" class="auto" height="240" src="/data/images/Students-at-Graduation.jpg" width="360"/>
       <h2 style="margin-top: .5em;">
        Alumni
       </h2>
       <p>
        Among Samford’s 52,838 alumni have included more than 60 members of the U.S. Congress, 8 state governors, 2 U.S. Supreme Court justices, a Secretary of State, 4 Rhodes Scholars, multiple Emmy and Grammy award-winning artists, 2 national championship football coaches, and recipients of the Pulitzer and Nobel Peace prizes.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="/alumni/">
         Samford Alumni
        </a>
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="fourcol last">
       <img alt="Students at a home football game" class="auto" height="320" src="/athletics/images/Bulldog-Country-DR10195633.jpg" width="480"/>
       <h2 style="margin-top: .5em;">
        Athletics
       </h2>
       <p>
        The university fields 17 varsity sports—eight men’s and nine women’s—that participate at the NCAA Division I level in the Southern Conference. Ranked first among Division I teams in Alabama and 17th in the nation, Samford’s athletic teams earned a 97% Graduation Success Rate (GSR) by the NCAA.
       </p>
