        <h2 id="domain" style="text-align: left;">
         1. Choose a Domain Name
        </h2>
        <hr/>
        <p>
         In order to build a website, the very first thing you’ll need is a domain name.
        </p>
        <p>
         The domain name is your website name and address. That address is used by visitors when they try to find your site through their web browsers.
        </p>
        <p>
         This website’s domain name is
         <strong>
          websitesetup.org
         </strong>
         . Yours can be anything.
        </p>
        <p>
         Domain names can cost anywhere from $10 to $50 a year. The usual price tag is around $15.
        </p>
        <p>
         If you haven’t registered or chosen a domain name for your website, here are some tips to help you out:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          If you’re making a
          <strong>
           website for a business
          </strong>
          , your domain name should match your company name. For example:
          <strong>
           YourCompanyName.com
          </strong>
         </li>
         <li>
          If you’re planning to set up a
          <strong>
           personal website
          </strong>
          for yourself then
          <strong>
           YourName.com
          </strong>
          can be a great option.
         </li>
         <li>
          Use a “generic” domain name extension such as
          <strong>
           .com
          </strong>
          ,
          <strong>
           .net
          </strong>
          and
          <strong>
           .org
          </strong>
          if your goal is international or US visitors. Use a “local” domain name extension such as
          <strong>
           .de
          </strong>
          ,
          <strong>
           .r
          </strong>
          or
          <strong>
           .ru
          </strong>
          if your goal is country-targeted visitors.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Don’t worry if your preferred domain name is already taken. There are more than 300 million different domain names registered, but there are a billion other possibilities that are still available.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          If you don’t have a domain name yet, there’s no need to register one.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          We’ll show you how to get a domain name for free for the first year (step 2):
         </strong>
        </p>

        <ol>
         <li>
          Click on the “Customize Fonts” link in the “Appearance → Neve Options” panel.
         </li>
         <li>
          Neve lets you pick from a whole catalog of system fonts and Google Fonts.
         </li>
         <li>
          Just click on the “Font Family” box and select the font you like.
          <img alt="change fonts" class="shadow-2 aligncenter wp-image-30141 size-full" height="834" loading="lazy" src="https://websitesetup.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/01/neve-customize-fonts-january-2021.jpg" width="541"/>
         </li>
         <li>
          After that, you can fine-tune the individual fonts used for your headings.
         </li>
        </ol>
        <p>
         Experiment with these settings to see the effect they have on your site.
        </p>
        <ol>
         <li>
          To customize the font used for the body section of your site, click on the arrow button near the top left.
          <img alt="customizing typograph" class="shadow-2 aligncenter wp-image-30142 size-full" height="292" loading="lazy" src="https://websitesetup.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/01/customizing-typography-january-2021.jpg" width="428"/>
         </li>
         <li>
          Click on “General.” This will take you to a similar options panel, but this time you’re adjusting the main body font.
         </li>
         <li>
          Click on “Publish” and “X” when done.
         </li>
        </ol>
        <h3>
         6) Add a Sidebar
        </h3>
        <p>
         Another thing you can do in the “Appearance → Neve Options” panel is set how you’d like your sidebar to look. Click on “Content / Sidebar” to begin.
        </p>
        <p>
         You have three main options here:
         <em>
          no sidebar
         </em>
         ,
         <em>
          sidebar to the left
         </em>
         , or
         <em>
          sidebar to the right
         </em>
         . The sidebar to the right is the classic layout for most websites. You can also set the content width. After doing your changes, click on “Publish” and “X.”
        </p>
        <h3>
         7) Add Widgets (Free WordPress Add-Ons)
        </h3>
        <p>
         Widgets are those small content blocks that usually appear in websites’ sidebars. Since we’ve just set the sidebar in the previous step, let’s now customize it with widgets.
        </p>
        <p>
         To configure widgets, go to “Appearance → Widgets.” You’ll see this:
         <span style="color: #ff0000;">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="wordpress widgets" class="shadow-2 aligncenter wp-image-30144 size-full" height="563" loading="lazy" src="https://websitesetup.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/01/wordpress-widgets-january-2021.jpg" width="1384"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         On the left, you can see all the widgets available; and on the right, there are all the widget areas supported by your current theme. To add a widget to the sidebar, all you need to do is grab the widget from the left and drag and drop it onto the sidebar area.
        </p>
        <p>
         For example, if you want to list all your pages in the sidebar, grab the “Pages” widget and drag it onto the sidebar section. You can also configure some basic settings of the widget.
        </p>
        <h3>
         8) Experiment With the Theme You Built
        </h3>
        <p>
         What we’ve covered above will be enough for you to figure out how to create a website that stands out and looks original, but there are many more options available.
        </p>
        <p>
         To see them all, go to “Appearance → Customize.” This will fire up the main interface of the Customizer with all its settings and presets.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="How to create a website: customizer" class="shadow-2 aligncenter wp-image-30145 size-full" height="645" loading="lazy" src="https://websitesetup.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/01/neve-customizer-january-2021.jpg" width="1384"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         We encourage you to browse through what’s available there and play around with some of the options. You’ll likely stumble upon some customizations we haven’t discussed here.
        </p>
        <h2 id="pages">
         5. Add Content/Pages to Your Website
        </h2>
        <hr/>
        <p>
         Pages are the essence of your website. It’s hard to imagine a website without any pages on it, right?
        </p>
        <p>
         From a technical point of view, a web page is just a document on the web. It’s not very different from your traditional Word document apart from it having your website’s branding elements such as the header and footer.
        </p>
        <p>
         Web pages are easy to create in WordPress. But before we get into the how-to, let’s discuss what pages you should create in the first place.
        </p>
        <p>
         Most websites will find the following pages essential:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Homepage
          </strong>
          – it’s the first page that your visitors see when they go to your website
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           About
          </strong>
          <strong>
           page
          </strong>
          – a page explaining what your website is about
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Contact
          </strong>
          <strong>
           page
          </strong>
          – a page letting visitors contact you
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Blog
          </strong>
          <strong>
           page
          </strong>
          – a listing of your most recent blog posts; if you’re not planning on blogging, you can use the blog page as a place for your company news and announcements
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Services
          </strong>
          <strong>
           page
          </strong>
          – if the website you’re building is for a business, use this page to showcase your services
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Shop
          </strong>
          <strong>
           page
          </strong>
          – for companies that want to launch an eCommerce store
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Many of the pages above will be very similar in structure – the only difference being the content on the actual page. Basically, once you learn how to create one page, you’ll know how to create them all. With that, there is some nuance here, so let’s now cover how to create a couple of basic types of pages:
        </p>
        <h3>
         1) Creating a Homepage
        </h3>
        <p>
         If you’re using Neve, your homepage looks something like this:
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Homepage" class="shadow-2 aligncenter wp-image-30146 size-full" height="713" loading="lazy" src="https://websitesetup.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/01/neve-homepage-january-2021.jpg" width="1384"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         You can edit the structure of this page as well as the elements on it (all texts and images).
        </p>
        <p>
         To do that, just click on the “Edit with Elementor” button in the top bar.
         <span style="color: #ff0000;">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="edit with Elementor" class="shadow-2 aligncenter wp-image-30159 size-full" height="196" loading="lazy" src="https://websitesetup.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/01/edit-with-elementor-january-2021.jpg" width="1384"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         What you’ll see is the interface of the Elementor page builder.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="How to create a website: Elementor" class="shadow-2 aligncenter wp-image-30160 size-full" height="651" loading="lazy" src="https://websitesetup.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/01/elementor-page-builder-january-2021.jpg" width="1384"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Elementor is what we call a
         <em>
          visual page builder
         </em>
         . This means that you can click any element that you see on the page and edit it directly. For example, if you want to change the main headline, just click on it and start typing.
         <span style="color: #ff0000;">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="headline type" class="shadow-2 aligncenter wp-image-30161 size-full" height="188" loading="lazy" src="https://websitesetup.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/01/elementor-edit-headline-january-2021.jpg" width="1384"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         If you take a look at the left sidebar, there’s a whole customization panel there. You can adjust the structure and the styling of any content block. For example, here’s what you can see when working on the headline:
         <span style="color: #ff0000;">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="headline editor" class="shadow-2 aligncenter wp-image-30162 size-full" height="601" loading="lazy" src="https://websitesetup.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/01/elementor-editor-left-sidebar-january-2021.jpg" width="1384"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Another cool thing is that you can grab onto any element on the page, and drag-and-drop it somewhere else.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Elementor blocks" class="shadow-2 wp-image-30164 size-full alignright" height="583" loading="lazy" src="https://websitesetup.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/01/elementor-content-blocks-january-2021.jpg" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         To add new elements, click on the small square icon in the top left corner.
        </p>
        <p>
         You’ll see a list of all the content blocks available.
        </p>
        <p>
         Grab any of the blocks and drag them onto the page’s canvas.
        </p>
        <p>
         The best way to work on your homepage is to go block by block and edit the demo content that’s on the page.
        </p>
        <p>
         Type in new text content, realign some of the blocks, delete the ones you don’t need, and add new ones accordingly.
        </p>
        <p>
         This whole interface is quite intuitive to use, so just spend an hour or so using it and experimenting with different things.
        </p>
        <h3>
         2) Create Pages Such as “About”, “Services”, “Contact”
        </h3>
        <p>
         Creating classic web pages is even easier to do in WordPress than working on your homepage. Go into “Pages → Add New.” You’ll see this interface:
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="How to create a website: add page" class="shadow-2 aligncenter wp-image-30147 size-full" height="564" loading="lazy" src="https://websitesetup.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/01/add-new-page-january-2021.jpg" width="1384"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Every page needs a title, so start by adding one where it says “Add a title.” For example, “About Us” or “Contact.”
        </p>
        <p>
         Next, pick a page layout. If you’re creating a standard page, you can go with the first one on the list – “Single Row.” But to speed things up, you can choose from the template library. For that, click on the blue button.
        </p>
        <p>
         You’ll see a window letting you pick from a range of pre-made page sections. If you’re working on your
         <em>
          about
         </em>
         page, for example, you can quickly find and use the following blocks:
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="about page blocks" class="shadow-2 aligncenter wp-image-30148 size-full" height="729" loading="lazy" src="https://websitesetup.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/01/neve-about-us-page-templates-january-2021.jpg" width="1384"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Just click on the “Insert” button next to the block you like, and it will be added to the page. From there, you can further customize it, change the texts, or replace images.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="about page edit" class="shadow-2 aligncenter wp-image-30149 size-full" height="805" loading="lazy" src="https://websitesetup.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/01/neve-about-us-editor-january-2021.jpg" width="1384"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         When you’re done, click on the “Publish” button in the top right corner of the screen.
        </p>
        <p>
         You can follow the same process when working on your
         <em>
          contact
         </em>
         page or
         <em>
          services
         </em>
         page as well.
        </p>
        <p>
         Just pick different page blocks from the template library. For example, these should work great on a
         <em>
          services
         </em>
         page:
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="services blocks" class="shadow-2 aligncenter wp-image-30150 size-full" height="815" loading="lazy" src="https://websitesetup.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/01/neve-services-pages-templates-january-2021.jpg" width="1384"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         If you want to add new page elements by hand – instead of using the templates – click on the “+” icon that’s in the top left corner of the editor interface.
         <span style="color: #ff0000;">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="add block" class="shadow-2 aligncenter wp-image-30151 size-full" height="584" loading="lazy" src="https://websitesetup.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/01/add-page-elements-manually-january-2021.jpg" width="1384"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Any block you select there will be added at the bottom of your page. Here’s what a new heading block looks like:
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="heading block example" class="shadow-2 aligncenter wp-image-30152 size-full" height="256" loading="lazy" src="https://websitesetup.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/01/heading-block-example-january-2021.jpg" width="1384"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         As you can see, you can edit it freely, change the properties of the text, plus there are color settings in the right sidebar.
        </p>
        <p>
         As always, remember to click on “Publish” when you’re done.
        </p>
        <h3>
         3) Create a Blog Page
        </h3>
        <p>
         The blog page is where the listing of your most recent blog posts can be found.
        </p>
        <p>
         The good news is that the blog page has already been created for you. This happened when you installed WordPress and the Neve theme. You can see that page by going to “Pages”:
         <span style="color: #ff0000;">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="blog page listing" class="shadow-2 aligncenter wp-image-30154 size-full" height="328" loading="lazy" src="https://websitesetup.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/01/blog-page-january-2021.jpg" width="1384"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Click on the “View” link to see your blog page in action.
        </p>
        <h4>
         Adding new blog posts:
        </h4>
        <p>
         To add new blog posts, simply go to Go to “Posts → Add New.”
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="add post" class="shadow-2 aligncenter wp-image-30155 size-full" height="439" loading="lazy" src="https://websitesetup.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/01/add-new-blog-post-january-2021.jpg" width="1384"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Does this interface remind you of anything? Yes, this is the same editing environment that we used when creating standard pages. Easy!
        </p>
        <h2 id="navigation">
         6. Set Up a Navigation Menu
        </h2>
        <hr/>
        <p>
         With a range of good-looking pages created, the next step in our quest to how to create a website is to set up your navigation (a website menu). The menu is what your visitors will use to go from page to page on your site.
        </p>
        <p>
         Go to “Appearance → Menus,” and from there, click on “create a new menu.”
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="How to create a website: create menu" class="shadow-2 aligncenter wp-image-30156 size-full" height="512" loading="lazy" src="https://websitesetup.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/01/create-a-new-menu-january-2021.jpg" width="1384"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Here’s how to create your first menu step by step:
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="WordPress Menu Creation Process" class="shadow-2 aligncenter wp-image-30157 size-full" height="648" loading="lazy" src="https://websitesetup.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/01/add-new-menu-step-by-step-january-2021.jpg" width="1384"/>
        </p>
        <ol>
         <li>
          Start by adding a title. The menu title doesn’t really matter, but it helps to recognize your menu among other menus (yes, you can have more than one).
         </li>
         <li>
          Select the pages to be added to your menu. You can also add other items to the menu – like posts or custom links.
         </li>
         <li>
          Click on the “Add to Menu” button to add the selected pages to your menu.
         </li>
         <li>
          Drag-and-drop menu items to arrange the links in order of importance.
         </li>
         <li>
          Select a menu display location. This differs from theme to theme, in this case, set your menu to the “Primary” location will display the menu in the header.
         </li>
         <li>
          Lastly, save your menu.
         </li>
        </ol>
        <p>
         At this stage, you’ll see your menu on the homepage in all its glory.
        </p>
        <h2 id="store">
         7. Add an Online Store/eCommerce
        </h2>
        <hr/>
        <p>
         The last thing we’ll do in this guide is to build ourselves an online store.
        </p>
        <p>
         If you wish to add an online store to your newly created website,
         <a href="https://websitesetup.org/woocommerce-online-store-tutorial/">
          <strong>
           follow this step-by-step tutorial here
          </strong>
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         This is obviously not something that all websites need, so feel free to skip this step if eCommerce isn’t something you want to get yourself into.
        </p>
        <h3>
         How WordPress eCommerce Works
        </h3>
        <p>
         With WordPress being such a versatile website platform, it’s no surprise that it can let you build a fully functional eCommerce online store.
         <em>
          By fully functional
         </em>
         , we mean that you can list any number of products, make them available for sale, and then also collect orders from customers and even handle all tax- and shipping-related elements of the whole process.
        </p>
        <p>
         This is all done with a plugin called WooCommerce.
        </p>
        <p>
         WooCommerce is the most popular, most functional, and frankly the best eCommerce solution for WordPress.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          We have a whole separate guide on how to get WooCommerce running on your WordPress website. We invite you to hop over there and read a
          <a href="https://websitesetup.org/woocommerce-online-store-tutorial/">
           detailed installation how-to
          </a>
          .
          <strong>
           Follow Steps 3 and 4
          </strong>
          and then come back here.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         … Okay, so with WooCommerce installed on your site and products added to your catalog, you’ll notice that, in the process, new pages have been created by WooCommerce automatically. Those pages are:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <strong>
           “Cart”
          </strong>
          – your store’s shopping cart
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           “Checkout”
          </strong>
          – the checkout page where customers can complete their purchases
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           “My account”
          </strong>
          – each customer’s profile; holds past orders, current details, and other personal information; the customer can always edit their info
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           “Shop” / “Products”
          </strong>
          – the main shop page – the one where your products are listed
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Each of these pages serves a specific purpose for your store and its functionality. The good news is that most themes these days are optimized to make those pages look right. The Neve theme is no different. If you visit any of these new pages, you’ll see that the presentation is clear and everything is easy to grasp. Here’s an example of the shopping cart page:
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="cart" class="shadow-2 aligncenter wp-image-15984" height="499" loading="lazy" sizes="(max-width: 800px) 100vw, 800px" src="https://websitesetup.org/wp-content/uploads/2020/01/cart-1024x639-1.jpg" srcset="https://websitesetup.org/wp-content/uploads/2020/01/cart-1024x639-1.jpg 1024w, https://websitesetup.org/wp-content/uploads/2020/01/cart-1024x639-1-640x399.jpg 640w, https://websitesetup.org/wp-content/uploads/2020/01/cart-1024x639-1-768x479.jpg 768w" width="800"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Apart from that, all these pages work like any other page on your WordPress website. This means that you can edit them, add your own elements, or change things like colors, layouts, etc. But when you’re doing this, be careful not to erase the WooCommerce shortcodes that are already there.
        </p>
        <p>
         You’ll also notice a new cart icon on the main menu of your site.
        </p>
        <p>
         At this stage, your eCommerce store is fully operational. This means that your customers can come in and do their shopping. You’ll see their orders in the WordPress admin area under “WooCommerce → Orders.”
         <span style="color: #ff0000;">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="orders" class="shadow-2 aligncenter wp-image-15985" height="271" loading="lazy" sizes="(max-width: 800px) 100vw, 800px" src="https://websitesetup.org/wp-content/uploads/2020/01/orders-1024x347-1.jpg" srcset="https://websitesetup.org/wp-content/uploads/2020/01/orders-1024x347-1.jpg 1024w, https://websitesetup.org/wp-content/uploads/2020/01/orders-1024x347-1-640x217.jpg 640w, https://websitesetup.org/wp-content/uploads/2020/01/orders-1024x347-1-768x260.jpg 768w" width="800"/>
        </p>
