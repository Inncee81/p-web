     <header class="win__headline">
      <span class="win__headline-line win__headline-line--large">
       A Presidency
      </span>
      <span class="win__headline-line win__headline-line--small">
       for
      </span>
      <span class="win__headline-line win__headline-line--large">
       All Americans
      </span>
     </header>
     <section class="win__hero-wrap">
      <div class="win__hero-img-wrap">
       <a class="win__hero-img-link mediabox" href="https://youtu.be/xJc_SRsbGS0">
        <noscript>
         <img class="bfp__hero-img win__hero-img" src="https://joebiden.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/11/America_the_beautiful.00_03_03_06.Still003.jpg"/>
        </noscript>
        <img class="lazyload bfp__hero-img win__hero-img" data-src="https://joebiden.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/11/America_the_beautiful.00_03_03_06.Still003.jpg" src="data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns=%22http://www.w3.org/2000/svg%22%20viewBox=%220%200%20210%20140%22%3E%3C/svg%3E"/>

     <section class="bfp__margin--large win__letter-body-wrap">
      <div class="bfp__margin--large win__letter-wrap">
       <h2 class="win__letter-header">
        My fellow Americans —
       </h2>
       <div class="bfp__copy win__letter-content">
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          The people of this nation have spoken.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          They have delivered us a clear victory. A convincing victory.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          A victory for “We the People.”
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          We have won with the most votes ever cast for a presidential ticket in the history of this nation — 81 million.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          I am humbled by the trust and confidence you have placed in me.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          I pledge to be a President who seeks not to divide, but to unify.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          Who doesn’t see Red and Blue states, but a United States.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          And who will work with all my heart to win the confidence of the whole people.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          For that is what America is about: The people.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          And that is what our Administration will be about.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          I sought this office to restore the soul of America.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          To rebuild the backbone of the nation — the middle class.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          To make America respected around the world again and to unite us here at home.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          It is the honor of my lifetime that so many millions of Americans have voted for this vision.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          And now the work of making this vision real is the task of our time.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          As I said many times before, I’m Jill’s husband.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          I would not be here without the love and tireless support of Jill, Hunter, Ashley, all of our grandchildren and their spouses, and all our family.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          They are my heart.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          Jill’s a mom — a military mom — and an educator.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          She has dedicated her life to education, but teaching isn’t just what she does — it’s who she is.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          For America’s educators, this is a great day: You’re going to have one of your own in the
         </span>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          White House, and Jill is going to make a great First Lady.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          And I will be honored to be serving with a fantastic vice president — Kamala Harris — who will make history as the first woman, first Black woman, first woman of South Asian descent, and first daughter of immigrants ever elected to national office in this country.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          It’s long overdue, and we’re reminded tonight of all those who fought so hard for so many years to make this happen. But once again, America has bent the arc of the moral universe towards justice.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          Kamala, Doug — like it or not — you’re family. You’ve become honorary Bidens and there’s no way out.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          To all those who volunteered, worked the polls in the middle of this pandemic, local election officials — you deserve a special thanks from this nation.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          To my campaign team, and all the volunteers, to all those who gave so much of themselves to make this moment possible, I owe you everything.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          And to all those who supported us: I am proud of the campaign we built and ran. I am proud of the coalition we put together, the broadest and most diverse in history.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          Democrats, Republicans and Independents.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          Progressives, moderates and conservatives.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          Young and old.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          Urban, suburban and rural.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          Gay, straight, transgender.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          White. Latino. Asian. Native American.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          And especially for those moments when this campaign was at its lowest — the African American community stood up again for me. They always have my back, and I’ll have yours.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          I said from the outset I wanted a campaign that represented America, and I think we did that. Now that’s what I want the administration to look like.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          And to those who voted for President Trump, I understand your disappointment tonight.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          I’ve lost a couple of elections myself.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          But now, let’s give each other a chance.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          It’s time to put away the harsh rhetoric.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          To lower the temperature.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          To see each other again.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          To listen to each other again.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          To make progress, we must stop treating our opponents as our enemy.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          We are not enemies. We are Americans.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          The Bible tells us that to everything there is a season — a time to build, a time to reap, a time to sow. And a time to heal.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          This is the time to heal in America.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          Now that the campaign is over — what is the people’s will? What is our mandate?
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          I believe it is this: Americans have called on us to marshal the forces of decency and the forces of fairness. To marshal the forces of science and the forces of hope in the great battles of our time.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          The battle to control the virus.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          The battle to build prosperity.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          The battle to secure your family’s health care.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          The battle to achieve racial justice and root out systemic racism in this country.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          The battle to save the climate.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          The battle to restore decency, defend democracy, and give everybody in this country a fair shot.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          Our work begins with getting COVID under control.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          We cannot repair the economy, restore our vitality, or relish life’s most precious moments — hugging a grandchild, birthdays, weddings, graduations, all the moments that matter most to us — until we get this virus under control.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          On Monday, I will name a group of leading scientists and experts as Transition Advisors to help take the Biden-Harris COVID plan and convert it into an action blueprint that starts on January 20th, 2021.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          That plan will be built on a bedrock of science. It will be constructed out of compassion, empathy, and concern.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          I will spare no effort — or commitment — to turn this pandemic around.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          I ran as a proud Democrat. I will now be an American president. I will work as hard for those who didn’t vote for me — as those who did.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          Let this grim era of demonization in America begin to end — here and now.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          The refusal of Democrats and Republicans to cooperate with one another is not due to some mysterious force beyond our control.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          It’s a decision. It’s a choice we make.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          And if we can decide not to cooperate, then we can decide to cooperate. And I believe that this is part of the mandate from the American people. They want us to cooperate.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          That’s the choice I’ll make. And I call on the Congress — Democrats and Republicans alike — to make that choice with me.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          The American story is about the slow, yet steady widening of opportunity.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          Make no mistake: Too many dreams have been deferred for too long.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          We must make the promise of the country real for everybody — no matter their race, their ethnicity, their faith, their identity, or their disability.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          America has always been shaped by inflection points — by moments in time where we’ve made hard decisions about who we are and what we want to be.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          Lincoln in 1860 — coming to save the Union.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          FDR in 1932 — promising a beleaguered country a New Deal.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          JFK in 1960 — pledging a New Frontier.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          And twelve years ago — when Barack Obama made history — and told us, “Yes, we can.”
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          We stand again at an inflection point.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          We have the opportunity to defeat despair and to build a nation of prosperity and purpose.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          We can do it. I know we can.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          I’ve long talked about the battle for the soul of America.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          We must restore the soul of America.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          Our nation is shaped by the constant battle between our better angels and our darkest impulses.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          It is time for our better angels to prevail.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          Tonight, the whole world is watching America. I believe at our best America is a beacon for the globe.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          And we lead not by the example of our power, but by the power of our example.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          I’ve always believed we can define America in one word: Possibilities.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          That in America everyone should be given the opportunity to go as far as their dreams and God-given ability will take them.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          You see, I believe in the possibility of this country.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          We’re always looking ahead.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          Ahead to an America that’s freer and more just.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          Ahead to an America that creates jobs with dignity and respect.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          Ahead to an America that cures disease — like cancer and Alzheimers.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          Ahead to an America that never leaves anyone behind.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          Ahead to an America that never gives up, never gives in.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          This is a great nation.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          And we are a good people.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          This is the United States of America.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          And there has never been anything we haven’t been able to do when we’ve done it together.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          In the last days of the campaign, I’ve been thinking about a hymn that means a lot to me and to my family, particularly my deceased son Beau. It captures the faith that sustains me and which I believe sustains America.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          And I hope it can provide some comfort and solace to the more than 230,000 families who have lost a loved one to this terrible virus this year. My heart goes out to each and every one of you. Hopefully this hymn gives you solace as well.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          “And He will raise you up on eagle’s wings,
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          Bear you on the breath of dawn,
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          Make you to shine like the sun,
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          And hold you in the palm of His Hand.”
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          And now, together — on eagle’s wings — we embark on the work that God and history have called upon us to do.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          With full hearts and steady hands, with faith in America and in each other, with a love of country — and a thirst for justice — let us be the nation that we know we can be.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          A nation united.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          A nation strengthened.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          A nation healed.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          The United States of America.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          God bless you.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400;">
          And may God protect our troops.
         </span>
        </p>
