            <h2>
             <span style="color:#003a5e;">
              What is mRNA?
             </span>
            </h2>
            <p>
             <span style="color:#444444;">
              Messenger RNA, or mRNA, plays a fundamental role in human biology, transferring the instructions stored in DNA to make the proteins required in every living cell. Our approach is to use mRNA medicines to instruct a patient’s own cells to produce proteins that could prevent, treat, or cure disease.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <a class="button-red" href="/mrna-technology/science-mrna">
              Read more about mRNA science
             </a>
            </p>
           </div>
          </div>
         </div>

            <img alt="" height="440" src="https://www.modernatx.com/sites/default/files/Bearwalk_Moderna_Website_2018_141.JPG" typeof="foaf:Image" width="660"/>
           </div>
           <div class="field field-name-field-featured-video-caption field-type-text-long field-label-hidden field-wrapper">
            <h3>
             The Science Behind mRNA Medicines
            </h3>
            <p>
             In using mRNA to develop medicines, we take advantage of normal biological processes to create a desired therapeutic effect. This gives us the potential to treat a broad spectrum of diseases, many of which cannot be addressed with current therapies.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a class="button-red" href="/mrna-technology/our-mrna-platform">
              Read more about our mRNA Platform
             </a>
            </p>

             <h2>
              Modalities
             </h2>
             <p>
              We refer to a modality as a family of potential mRNA medicines, consistent both in design and how and where they are delivered in the body. Modalities may focus on different diseases within one therapeutic area - or across therapeutic areas - allowing us to potentially use one technological solution to create medicines for many diseases.
             </p>
