        <font class="Subscription">

        </font>
        ] Clarifying memory management with page folios
       </h2>
       <div class="BlurbListing">
        <font size="-1">
         [Kernel] Posted Mar 18, 2021 16:33 UTC (Thu) by corbet
        </font>
        <p>
         Memory management generally works at the level of pages, which typically
contain 4,096 bytes but may be larger.  The kernel, though, has extended
the concept of pages to include compound pages, which are groups of
contiguous single pages.  That, in turn, has made the definition of
what a "page" is a bit fuzzy.  Matthew Wilcox has been working since last
year on a concept called "page folios" which is meant to bring the picture
back into focus; whether the memory-management community will accept it
remains unclear, though.

        The linux.dev mailing-list service launches
       </h2>
       <div class="BlurbListing">
        <font size="-1">
         [Kernel] Posted Mar 15, 2021 20:34 UTC (Mon) by corbet
        </font>
        <p>
         There is
         <a href="https://www.kernel.org/lists-linux-dev.html">
          a new
mailing-list server
         </a>
         running under the auspices of kernel.org that is
meant, over time, to address the problems that have been plaguing
vger.kernel.org in recent times.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <blockquote class="bq">
         The infrastructure behind
lists.linux.dev supports multiple domains, so all mailing lists hosted on
vger.kernel.org will be carefully migrated to the same platform while
preserving current addresses, subscribers, and list ids. The only thing
that will noticeably change is the procedure to subscribe and unsubscribe
from individual lists.
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         Among other things, the new server
prioritizes delivery to the lore.kernel.org archive, which should minimize
the problems seen recently with lost messages.
