            About NASP
           </h2>
           <p>
            The National Association of School Psychologists (NASP) represents and supports the profession of school psychology by advancing effective practices to improve students' learning, behavior and mental health, and by maintaining essential standards for ethics and practice.

            NASP Practice Model
           </h3>
           <div class="margin-large formatted">
            <p style="display: none;">
             <img alt="" class="img-right" src="Images/content-practice-model.jpg"/>
             The
             <a href="Documents/Standards and Certification/Standards/2_PracticeModel.pdf">
              NASP
              <em>
               Model for Comprehensive and Integrated School Psychological Services
              </em>
             </a>
             (PDF), also known as the
             <strong>
              NASP Practice Model
             </strong>
             , represents NASP's official policy regarding the delivery of school psychological services. NASP has developed
             <a href="/standards-and-certification/nasp-practice-model/nasp-practice-model-implementation-guide">
              this implementation guide
             </a>
             as a resource to help school psychologists through the process of advocating for and implementing the model in their school or district.
            </p>

             <img alt="Practice Model" class="img-left" height="200" src="Images/Standards and Certification/2019-Practice-Model-NEW.png" width="200"/>

             <em>
              NASP Practice Model
             </em>
             , represents NASP’s official policy regarding the delivery of school psychological services. The model was updated as part of the NASP 2020 Professional Standards revision and builds on the success of the 2010 standards in advancing the comprehensive role of school psychologists across the country.
