                   Avi Wigderson Awarded 2021 Abel Prize
                  </a>
                 </h3>
                 <div class="node--grid__detail">
                  <div class="field field--name-field-post-date field--type-datetime field--label-hidden">
                   <div class="field__item">
                    <time class="datetime" datetime="2021-03-17T12:00:00Z">

                 <div class="node--grid__content field-ias-teaser">
                  <p>
                   <a href="https://www.ias.edu/scholars/wigderson">
                    Avi Wigderson
                   </a>
                   of the Institute for Advanced Study (IAS) was named a recipient of the 2021 Abel Prize, which he shares jointly with
                   <a href="https://www.ias.edu/scholars/l%C3%A1szl%C3%B3-lov%C3%A1sz">
                    László Lovász
                   </a>

                   Avi Wigderson and the Second Golden Era of Theoretical Computing
                  </a>
                 </h3>
                 <div class="node--grid__detail">
                  <div class="field field--name-field-ias-multi-author-byline">
                  </div>
                 </div>
                 <div class="node--grid__content field-ias-teaser">
                  <p>
                   Avi Wigderson joined the IAS Faculty in 1999, launching what IAS Director Robbert Dijkgraaf has called the “second golden era of theoretical computing” at the Institute.
                  </p>

                 <h3 class="node--grid__label">
                  <a href="/covid-19-updates">
                   Covid-19 Updates
                  </a>
                 </h3>
                 <div class="node--grid__content field-ias-teaser">
                  <p>
                   <strong>
                    CAMPUS REOPENING
                   </strong>
                   <br/>
                   As of September 8, the Institute campus has partially reopened, and Faculty, Members, and a limited number of essential staff may request access to work in their offices. The
                   <strong>
                    <a href="https://forms.ias.edu/form/health-screening">
                     Health Screening Form
                    </a>
                   </strong>
                   must be completed each morning before coming to campus and approved by the IAS Covid-19 Response Team.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   * At this time, the Institute's academic campus is accessible
                   <strong>
                    only
                   </strong>
                   to the IAS community, and is not open to nonessential visitors, members of the public, or family members, including children.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   We have created a series of
                   <strong>
                    <a href="/return-campus-guides">
                     Return-to-Campus guides
                    </a>
                   </strong>
                   that contain more information and should be consulted before coming to campus.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <strong>
                    TRAVEL
                   </strong>
                   <br/>
                   Travelers and residents entering New Jersey from
                   <strong>
                    any U.S. state or territory
                   </strong>
                   beyond the immediate region (New York, Connecticut, Pennsylvania, and Delaware) should self-quarantine for 10 days. Individuals who test negative on a viral test for Covid-19 must self-quarantine for 7 full days. For the most up-to-date information, view
                   <a href="https://covid19.nj.gov/faqs/nj-information/travel-and-transportation/which-states-are-on-the-travel-advisory-list-are-there-travel-restrictions-to-or-from-new-jersey">
                    New Jersey Covid FAQs
                   </a>
                   .
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   Additionally, individuals who leave the state of New Jersey for more than 24 hours must complete the IAS
                   <a href="https://forms.ias.edu/form/travel-questionnaire">
                    Travel Questionnaire
                   </a>
                   upon their return.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <strong>
                    FACE COVERINGS
                   </strong>
                   <br/>
                   The State of New Jersey’s mandate requires everyone to wear a face covering indoors, including common spaces, hallways, and libraries—even if you are the only person in the space. If you are working in a private office and another person enters, please put your face covering on and stay at least six feet apart. Outdoors, face coverings also should be worn if social distancing isn't possible.
                  </p>
