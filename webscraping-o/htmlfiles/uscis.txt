             <div class="ribbon-description">
              <div class="field field--name-field-announcement-description field__item">
               <p>
                We are no longer applying the August 2019 Public Charge Final Rule. On March 9, 2021, the U.S. Court of Appeals for the Seventh Circuit lifted its stay of the U.S. District Court for the Northern District of Illinois’ Nov. 2, 2020, decision which vacated the Inadmissibility on Public Charge Grounds final rule nationwide. USCIS immediately stopped applying the Public Charge Final Rule to all pending applications and petitions.
               </p>
               <p>
                For more information, see our main
                <a data-entity-substitution="canonical" data-entity-type="node" data-entity-uuid="d1ea706e-c7f2-4025-82e6-8b6330e5add4" href="/green-card/green-card-processes-and-procedures/public-charge" title="Public Charge">
                 public charge webpage
                </a>
                , our
                <a data-entity-substitution="canonical" data-entity-type="node" data-entity-uuid="daf856e3-e1ee-4f39-a12b-165c931c9cb9" href="/green-card/green-card-processes-and-procedures/public-charge/inadmissibility-on-public-charge-grounds-final-rule-litigation" title="Inadmissibility on Public Charge Grounds Final Rule: Litigation">
                 public charge litigation page
                </a>
                , and
                <a href="https://www.dhs.gov/news/2021/03/09/dhs-statement-litigation-related-public-charge-ground-inadmissibility">
                 DHS’s news release
                </a>
                .
               </p>
