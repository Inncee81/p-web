        <div class="embedded-content">
         <p>
          The International Astronomical Union (IAU) was founded in 1919. Its mission is to promote and safeguard the science of astronomy in all its aspects, including research, communication, education and development, through international cooperation. Its Individual and Junior Members — structured in
          <a href="/science/scientific_bodies/divisions/">
           Divisions
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="/science/scientific_bodies/commissions/">
           Commissions
          </a>
          , and
          <a href="/science/scientific_bodies/working_groups/">
           Working Groups
          </a>
          — are professional astronomers from all over the world, at the Ph.D. level and beyond, active in professional research and education in astronomy. The IAU has 11776
          <a href="/administration/membership/member/">
           Individual and Junior Members
          </a>
          in 107 countries worldwide. Of those countries 82 are
          <a href="/administration/membership/national/">
           National Members
          </a>
          . In addition, the IAU collaborates with various scientific organizations all over the world.
         </p>
