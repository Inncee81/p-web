            Legal considerations regarding claims for international protection made in the context of the adverse effects of climate change and disasters

          <h3>
           <a href="https://www.refworld.org/docid/5f1edee24.html">
            UNHCR Position on the Designations of Libya as a Safe Third Country and as a Place of Safety for the Purpose of Disembarkation Following Rescue at Sea
           </a>
          </h3>
          <p>
           UNHCR, the UN Refugee Agency released a Position on the Designations of Libya as a Safe Third Country and as a Place of Safety for the Purpose of Disembarkation Following Rescue at Sea, partially updating its 2018 Position on Returns to Libya.
          </p>

         <div class="item">

           26 August 2020
          </span>
          <h3>
           <a href="https://www.refworld.org/rsdproceduralstandards.html">
            Release of the 2020 RSD Procedural Standards
           </a>
          </h3>
          UNHCR has published a revised and updated 2020 version of the Procedural Standards for RSD under UNHCR's Mandate (RSD Procedural Standards), originally published in 2003, which set out core standards and best practices to ensure harmonised, efficient and quality UNHCR RSD procedures, including for reception and registration.

The full text of the 2020 RSD Procedural Standards can be found at:
          <a href="/docid/5e870b254.html">
           Procedural Standards for Refugee Status Determination Under UNHCR's Mandate
          </a>
          UN High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR), 26 August 2020
         </div>

          <h3>

            UNHCR Key Legal Considerations on access to territory for persons in need of international protection in the context of the COVID-19 response
           </a>
          </h3>
          On 16 March 2020, the UNHCR published key legal considerations regarding country responses to the COVID-19 pandemic.
         </div>
         <div class="item">
          <span>
           14 May 2020
          </span>
          <h3>
           <a href="https://www.refworld.org/faq.html">
            Refworld and ecoi.net are partnering up for a streamlined content division between the protection information databases
           </a>
          </h3>
          <p>
           The United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR) and the Austrian Centre for Country of Origin and Asylum Research and Documentation (ACCORD), a department of the Austrian Red Cross, have joined forces in providing protection information to asylum decision makers.
          </p>
          <p>
           As of 1 January 2019, ACCORD's ecoi.net will be endorsed by UNHCR as the main global platform for country of origin information. At the same time, UNHCR's Refworld will reinforce its law and policy collections in the course of 2019, relaunching its protection information platform in 2020.
          </p>
